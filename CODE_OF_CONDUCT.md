# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Hawkeye Sterling is a regulator-grade AML/CFT compliance platform. We — the
maintainers and contributors — pledge to make participation in this project a
harassment-free, professional experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality,
personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Because this project handles regulated financial-crime data, we hold ourselves
to an additional standard: **integrity, confidentiality, and professional
conduct are not optional.** A breach of confidentiality or the falsification of
compliance evidence is treated as a Code of Conduct violation of the most
serious kind.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and courtesy toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility, apologising to those affected by our mistakes, and
  learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its regulatory obligations.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or
  advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information — physical or email address — without
  explicit permission.
- **Disclosing regulated data** (customer PII, sanctions hits, SAR/STR content,
  internal case detail) in any issue, pull request, comment, commit, or channel.
- **Falsifying, backdating, or misrepresenting** compliance evidence, audit-chain
  entries, test results, or governance records.
- Deliberately weakening a security or compliance invariant (see `CLAUDE.md`)
  without an approved, documented governance decision.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The repository maintainer (acting as, or on behalf of, the MLRO) is responsible
for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behaviour and will take
appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behaviour deemed
inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

The maintainer has the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned
with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions
when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository, issues,
pull requests, discussions, and any linked collaboration tooling (e.g. Asana,
email) — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the
project in public spaces.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be
reported privately to the maintainer at **hawkeye.sterling.v2@gmail.com**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All
reporters' privacy and security will be respected.

> Suspected **security vulnerabilities** should follow [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md),
> not this process. Suspected disclosure of **regulated data** is both a Code of
> Conduct matter and a potential security incident — report it via
> [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md) and reference the incident runbook,
> [`docs/INCIDENT-RECOVERY.md`](./docs/INCIDENT-RECOVERY.md).

## Enforcement guidelines

The maintainer will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the
consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

| Level | Community impact | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Correction** | Inappropriate language or unprofessional behaviour. | A private, written warning with clarity around the nature of the violation and why it was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested. |
| **2. Warning** | A violation through a single incident or series of actions. | A warning with consequences for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. Further violation may lead to a ban. |
| **3. Temporary ban** | A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour. | A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the project for a specified period. |
| **4. Permanent ban** | Demonstrating a pattern of violation, harassment of an individual, aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals, **or a deliberate breach of regulated-data confidentiality or compliance-evidence integrity.** | A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project. |
