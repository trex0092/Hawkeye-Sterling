1.  RECORD RETENTION OBLIGATIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I understand the firm's obligation under Federal               [    ]
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 to retain all AML/CFT records
for a minimum of 10 years.

I know the scope of records covered by the retention           [    ]
obligation: CDD records, EDD records, transaction records,
sanctions screening logs, training records, STR and
supporting evidence, and MLRO reports.

I understand that FATF Recommendation 11 (record-keeping)      [    ]
requires the firm to maintain records that are sufficient
to permit reconstruction of individual transactions and
to provide evidence for prosecution of criminal activity.

I know that all compliance artefacts are archived as           [    ]
plain-text UTF-8 files in the firm's repository and that
this format is chosen for regulator transparency.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.  ARCHIVE INTEGRITY AND HASH MANIFEST
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I understand the purpose of the Archive Integrity Manifest     [    ]
and I know that it computes a SHA256 fingerprint of every
file in the history archive.

I know that the hash manifest is itself archived, creating     [    ]
a chain that allows any future inspection to verify the
integrity of the archive at any prior point in time.

I understand that a hash mismatch indicates that a file        [    ]
has been modified after the fact and that this must be
reported to the MLRO immediately.

I know that the screening engine's audit chain is              [    ]
append-only and tamper-evident, with each event linked to
the previous event by hash.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.  COMPLIANCE DASHBOARD
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I know that the Compliance Dashboard provides a                [    ]
traffic-light summary of the firm's 15 control cadences
(green, amber, red) and that it is generated
automatically from the archive data.

I understand what each colour means: green indicates the       [    ]
control is operating within tolerance, amber indicates a
minor gap or delay, and red indicates a material gap
requiring MLRO attention.

I can identify which dashboard indicators relate to my         [    ]
role and I know what action to take when an indicator
I am responsible for moves to amber or red.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.  REGULATORY MONITORING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I understand that the Regulatory Watcher monitors publicly     [    ]
available regulatory web pages (MOE, EOCN, CBUAE, FATF)
for changes that may affect the firm's AML/CFT programme.

I know that a changed source triggers a review by the          [    ]
MLRO to determine whether the firm's controls, risk
ratings or counterparty assessments need updating.

I understand that the firm monitors the FATF public            [    ]
statements and jurisdiction lists and that changes to
these lists may require immediate action on the
counterparty register.

I know that regulatory monitoring records are retained         [    ]
for 10 years and form part of the evidence the Ministry
of Economy may review during a supervisory inspection.
