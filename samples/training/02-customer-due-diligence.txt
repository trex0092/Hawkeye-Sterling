-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I understand the firm's obligation under Federal               [    ]
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 to apply Customer Due Diligence
at the opening of every customer relationship, at the
point of any occasional transaction above the firm's
internal threshold, and at periodic refresh intervals.

I know the difference between standard CDD, simplified         [    ]
CDD and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD), and I can identify
which level applies to a given customer.

I understand the CDD refresh windows applied by the firm:      [    ]
12 months for high and critical risk counterparties, 24
months for medium risk, and 36 months for low risk.

I know that the CDD Refresh Tracker report identifies          [    ]
overdue, critical, warning and approaching files, and I
understand my responsibility when a file appears in the
overdue category.

I am aware that FATF Recommendation 22 requires DNFBPs        [    ]
to apply customer due diligence measures and that the
firm's CDD process is designed to meet this requirement.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.  ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I know that Enhanced Due Diligence must be applied to          [    ]
all Politically Exposed Persons, to all counterparties
rated high or critical risk, and to all counterparties
in jurisdictions on the FATF increased monitoring list.

I understand that the Monthly EDD Case Tracker monitors        [    ]
the population of EDD files across all programme entities
and reports to the MLRO on their review status.

I can identify the circumstances under which a standard        [    ]
CDD file should be escalated to an EDD pathway, including
adverse media indicators, sanctions screening matches,
and unusual transaction patterns.

I know that the MLRO must personally review every EDD          [    ]
file and that the compliance function cannot discharge an
EDD review without the MLRO's sign-off.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.  COUNTERPARTY REGISTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I understand the purpose of the firm's counterparty            [    ]
register and I know what information it contains for each
counterparty (name, jurisdiction, risk rating, entities
touching, typologies, status, MLRO notes).

I know how to read the counterparty register and I can         [    ]
identify counterparties that are rated critical or high
risk, or that have cross-entity exposure.

I understand that the Quarterly Beneficial Ownership            [    ]
Clarity Report identifies corporate counterparties
without a recorded beneficial ownership note, and I know
the firm's obligation to obtain this information.

I am aware that the counterparty register is updated           [    ]
daily and archived under the firm's 10-year retention
policy in accordance with Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of
2025 and FATF Recommendation 11.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.  DECLINED ONBOARDINGS AND CUSTOMER EXITS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
                                                               Rating
                                                               ------
I know the circumstances under which the firm must             [    ]
decline a customer onboarding, including failure to
provide satisfactory identification, source of funds or
source of material evidence.

I understand the difference between a "cleared" exit           [    ]
(relationship ended by mutual agreement) and an
"escalated" exit (relationship terminated on compliance
grounds).

I know that the Annual Customer Exit Report records every      [    ]
exit and escalation, and that this report is reviewed by
the MLRO and retained for 10 years.

I understand that a declined onboarding or a refused           [    ]
transaction may require a filing with the Financial
Intelligence Unit, and that this decision rests with the
MLRO.
