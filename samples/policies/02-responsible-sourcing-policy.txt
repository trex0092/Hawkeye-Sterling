This policy applies to every director, officer, employee, contractor and
agent of [Reporting Entity] who is involved in the sourcing, purchasing,
refining, trading or selling of precious metals and precious stones, or
who performs any compliance or operational function touching the supply
chain.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The purpose of this policy is to set out the principles and practical
steps that [Reporting Entity] will follow to source precious metals and
precious stones responsibly. It exists to prevent the firm from
contributing, directly or through its supply chain, to money laundering,
terrorist financing, conflict financing, serious human rights abuses,
bribery or corruption.

This policy covers all materials that pass through the firm's custody
at every stage of the supply chain from mine or recycling source to
end customer. All staff must read, understand and apply it.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This policy rests on three foundations. First, the OECD Due Diligence
Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-
Affected and High-Risk Areas, which provides the internationally
accepted five-step framework for mineral supply chain due diligence.
Second, the LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance (RGG), version 9, which
translates the five OECD steps into requirements specific to gold
refiners and trading houses. Third, Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025
on Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism
and Financing of Illegal Organisations, the primary AML/CFT statute
in the United Arab Emirates. Where one framework is stricter than
another, the firm will follow the stricter standard.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS (STEP 1)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will maintain management systems that support responsible
sourcing. These include this policy document, approved by the Board
and communicated to every supplier and counterparty; a compliance
team led by the MLRO with authority to suspend or terminate any
relationship where supply chain risks cannot be managed; a supplier
due diligence procedure requiring know-your-counterparty information
before any new supply relationship and periodic refreshes based on
risk; a grievance mechanism through which employees, suppliers and
other stakeholders may raise sourcing concerns to the MLRO or through
the firm's confidential reporting channel; and a record-keeping system
that preserves all supply chain due diligence documentation for at
least ten years.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. RISK IDENTIFICATION (STEP 2)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will identify and assess supply chain risks on a continuing
basis by mapping the chain from extraction or recycling to the firm's
own operations, screening every counterparty against sanctions lists,
adverse media databases and internal watch lists at onboarding and at
least every six months, and identifying red flags such as materials
from conflict-affected and high-risk areas (CAHRAs), refusal to
provide origin documentation, unusually complex structures, pricing
that deviates from the market rate, and links to artisanal mining
operations where forced or child labour has been reported. Identified
risks will be recorded in a supply chain risk register maintained by
the MLRO and reviewed by the Board at least annually.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SUPPLY CHAIN DUE DILIGENCE (STEP 3)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Where Step 2 reveals actual or potential risks the firm will design
and carry out a response. Measures may include engaging the supplier
for additional origin and chain-of-custody information, requiring the
supplier to demonstrate its own OECD-aligned due diligence programme,
reviewing independent assurance reports or LBMA Good Delivery List
status for upstream refiners, suspending trade while the investigation
is ongoing, or terminating the relationship and filing a suspicious
transaction report with the Financial Intelligence Unit if the supply
chain is found to be linked to money laundering, terrorist financing,
conflict financing or serious human rights abuses. All mitigation
measures and their outcomes will be documented in the risk register.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. INDEPENDENT AUDIT (STEP 4)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will arrange an independent third-party audit of its supply
chain due diligence at least once every twelve months. The audit will
cover the design and effectiveness of the management systems in Step 1,
the completeness of the risk identification in Step 2, the adequacy
of the mitigation in Step 3 and the accuracy of the annual report in
Step 5. The MLRO will give the auditor unrestricted access to the risk
register, counterparty files and transaction records. Findings and
remediation recommendations will be presented to the Board within sixty
days of fieldwork completion and tracked at each subsequent Board
meeting until closed.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. ANNUAL REPORTING (STEP 5)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will produce an annual report on its responsible sourcing
activities covering, at a minimum, the management systems in place,
a summary of supply chain risks identified during the period, the
steps taken to mitigate those risks including any relationships
suspended or terminated, and the results of the independent audit.
The report will be approved by the Board, made available to the
Ministry of Economy upon request and retained for ten years.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. CAHRA IDENTIFICATION AND MONITORING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will maintain an up-to-date list of conflict-affected and
high-risk areas compiled from United Nations Security Council
resolutions, European Union restrictive measures, the FATF lists of
jurisdictions under increased monitoring and high-risk jurisdictions
subject to a call for action, the LBMA CAHRA list and credible non-
governmental and media reports. The MLRO will review the list at
least quarterly and communicate changes to the purchasing and trading
desks within two business days. Material originating from or transiting
through a CAHRA will be subject to enhanced due diligence before any
trade is permitted. If enhanced due diligence cannot provide reasonable
assurance that the material is conflict-free, the firm will not proceed.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. RECORD-KEEPING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

All records created under this policy will be retained for a minimum
of ten years from the date of the transaction or the date on which the
business relationship ended, whichever is later. Records include
supplier onboarding files, supply chain maps, risk assessments,
screening results, correspondence regarding origin and chain of
custody, audit reports, remediation plans and annual reports. Records
will be stored so that they can be retrieved promptly and provided to
the supervisory authority or law enforcement upon request.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. POLICY REVIEW
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This policy will be reviewed at least once every twelve months by the
MLRO, or sooner if there is a material change in the firm's business
model, supply chain, regulatory environment or risk landscape. Any
amendment requires Board approval. The MLRO will maintain a version
history log recording the date, nature and reason for each amendment.
