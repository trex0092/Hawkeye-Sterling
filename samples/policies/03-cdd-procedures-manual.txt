This manual sets out the customer due diligence (CDD) procedures that
every director, officer, employee, contractor and agent of
[Reporting Entity] must follow when establishing or maintaining a
business relationship or carrying out an occasional transaction.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The purpose of this manual is to provide step-by-step procedures for
identifying and verifying customers, understanding the nature of their
business, assessing the risks they present and keeping that information
current throughout the life of the relationship.

These procedures apply to every customer, whether a natural person, a
legal person or a legal arrangement, and to both one-off transactions
and continuing business relationships. No business relationship may be
established and no transaction may be executed until the applicable CDD
requirements have been completed to the satisfaction of the MLRO or,
for standard CDD, to the satisfaction of the assigned compliance
officer.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

These procedures are grounded in Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 on
Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism and
Financing of Illegal Organisations, together with its implementing
regulations. They also reflect FATF Recommendation 22, which requires
dealers in precious metals and stones to apply CDD when engaging in
cash transactions at or above the applicable threshold. Guidance from
the Ministry of Economy is incorporated as published. Where instruments
conflict, the firm applies the stricter requirement.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. STANDARD CDD REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Standard CDD is the baseline for every customer unless the risk
assessment indicates that simplified or enhanced measures are needed.

Natural persons. The firm will collect and verify the full legal name,
date and place of birth, nationality, country of residence, current
address, occupation and a clear copy of a valid government-issued
photo identification such as a passport or Emirates ID. Verification
will be against the original or a certified copy. Where the customer
is not physically present, electronic verification will be used and
the safeguards under enhanced due diligence will apply.

Legal persons. The firm will collect and verify the full legal name
and trading names, legal form and proof of existence such as a trade
licence, registered office and principal place of business, the names
of directors, the constitutional documents and the identity of
individuals authorised to act on behalf of the entity. Registration
details will be checked against the relevant commercial register.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. SIMPLIFIED DUE DILIGENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Simplified due diligence (SDD) may be applied only where the risk
assessment establishes that the customer, product, delivery channel
and geography all present demonstrably lower risk. SDD is not an
exemption; it means the extent or frequency of certain measures may
be reduced. The MLRO must approve SDD for any customer category before
it takes effect, documenting the measures reduced and the rationale.
Examples include transactions with regulated financial institutions in
jurisdictions with effective AML/CFT regimes and with UAE government
entities. Even where SDD applies the firm will identify the customer
and monitor for unusual activity. If the risk profile changes, the
customer will be moved to standard or enhanced due diligence.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Enhanced due diligence (EDD) applies whenever the risk assessment
identifies a higher risk of money laundering or terrorist financing.
EDD is mandatory in the following situations and may be applied at
the discretion of the MLRO to any other case.

Triggers. Customers who are politically exposed persons (PEPs) or
their family members or close associates. Customers from, or
transactions involving, jurisdictions on the FATF high-risk or
increased-monitoring lists. Non-face-to-face relationships. Complex
or unusually large transactions with no apparent economic or lawful
purpose. Correspondent relationships.

Politically exposed persons. When a customer, beneficial owner or
authorised signatory is a PEP, the firm will obtain senior management
approval, establish source of wealth and source of funds, and conduct
enhanced ongoing monitoring for the duration of PEP status and for a
reasonable period afterwards. PEP status is determined by screening
against commercially available databases and, where inconclusive, by
direct enquiry.

High-risk jurisdictions. When a customer is connected to a jurisdiction
on the FATF high-risk or increased-monitoring list, countermeasures
proportionate to the risk will be applied, including collecting
additional information, increasing monitoring frequency and requiring
the first payment through a regulated bank account in the customer's
name.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP IDENTIFICATION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

For every customer that is a legal person or legal arrangement, the
firm will identify and verify the beneficial owner, being any natural
person who ultimately owns or controls the customer or on whose behalf
a transaction is conducted. Ownership of twenty-five per cent or more
of shares, voting rights or capital is an indicator, but the firm will
look beyond formal structures. If no beneficial owner can be identified,
the firm will record the senior managing official and document the
steps taken. Verification will use independent sources such as
commercial registers and central beneficial ownership registers, not
solely the customer's self-declaration.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SOURCE OF FUNDS AND SOURCE OF WEALTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm will establish the source of funds for every transaction and
the source of wealth for the business relationship. Source of funds is
the origin of the specific funds used and will be evidenced by bank
statements, sale contracts, loan agreements or similar documents.
Source of wealth is the origin of the customer's overall financial
standing, covering occupation, business activities, investments,
inheritances and other material contributors. The depth of enquiry
will be proportionate to the risk profile. Where the customer is
unable or unwilling to provide satisfactory evidence, the firm will
not proceed and will consider whether a suspicious transaction report
should be filed with the Financial Intelligence Unit.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. ONGOING DUE DILIGENCE AND PERIODIC REVIEW
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transaction monitoring. The firm will monitor transactions to confirm
they are consistent with its knowledge of the customer and the stated
source of funds. Transactions that fall outside the expected pattern
will be investigated and documented.

Periodic review. CDD files will be reviewed and updated at intervals
set by the customer's risk rating: at least every twelve months for
high-risk customers, every twenty-four months for medium-risk and
every thirty-six months for low-risk.

Event-driven review. A review will also be triggered by a change in
ownership or control, a shift in transaction volume, adverse media or
screening alerts, or a request from law enforcement. Each review will
confirm that identification documents remain valid, beneficial
ownership is accurate, the risk rating is appropriate and there is no
reason to file a suspicious transaction report.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. CDD FAILURE AND RELATIONSHIP EXIT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

If the firm cannot complete CDD, it will not establish the relationship
or carry out the transaction. If the relationship already exists, it
will be terminated in an orderly manner. The MLRO will assess whether
the failure gives rise to a suspicion and, if so, will file a
suspicious transaction report with the Financial Intelligence Unit
before or at the time of termination. The reasons, steps taken and
filing decision will be documented and retained. The firm will not
alert the customer to the fact that a report has been or may be filed.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. RECORD-KEEPING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

All CDD records will be retained for ten years from the date of the
transaction or the end of the business relationship, whichever is
later. Records include identification documents, verification results,
beneficial ownership records, source of funds and source of wealth
documentation, risk assessments, monitoring records, review notes and
screening results. Records will be stored securely and retrievable
promptly for the supervisory authority, the Financial Intelligence
Unit or law enforcement upon request.
