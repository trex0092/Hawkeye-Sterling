1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  This document sets out the policy of [Reporting Entity] for the
     prevention of money laundering, the financing of terrorism, and
     the financing of illegal organisations. It applies to every
     director, officer, employee, agent, and third party acting on
     behalf of the firm across all services, business lines, and
     jurisdictions in which the firm operates.

1.2  The purpose of this policy is to establish clear obligations,
     controls, and procedures so that the firm identifies, assesses,
     mitigates, and manages its exposure to money laundering and
     terrorist financing risk. It is the master policy document to
     which all supporting procedures, guidelines, and operational
     manuals are subordinate.

1.3  Every person to whom this policy is addressed must read it in
     full, confirm understanding, and comply with it at all times.
     Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action and may
     expose the individual and the firm to criminal and regulatory
     sanctions.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  This policy is issued in compliance with the following laws,
     regulations, standards, and supervisory requirements:

     (a) Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 on Anti-Money Laundering
         and Combating the Financing of Terrorism and Illegal
         Organisations, together with all Cabinet Decisions and
         Implementing Regulations made thereunder.

     (b) FATF Recommendation 22 (Customer Due Diligence for Designated
         Non-Financial Businesses and Professions), Recommendation 23
         (Other Measures for DNFBPs), and Recommendation 28 (Regulation
         and Supervision of DNFBPs).

     (c) All directives, guidance, and circulars issued by the Ministry
         of Economy (MOE), acting as the supervisory authority for
         Designated Non-Financial Businesses and Professions (DNFBPs)
         in the United Arab Emirates.

     (d) Reporting obligations to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU)
         of the United Arab Emirates, including the requirement to file
         reports through the goAML platform.

     (e) Obligations arising from United Nations Security Council
         Resolutions, the UAE Local Terrorist List, and all notices
         issued by the Executive Office of the Committee for Goods and
         Materials Subject to Import and Export Control (EOCN) relating
         to targeted financial sanctions.

2.2  Where any provision of this policy conflicts with an applicable
     law or regulation, the law or regulation shall prevail, and the
     MLRO shall amend this policy accordingly without delay.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1  Board of Directors

     The Board carries ultimate responsibility for the firm's AML and
     CFT programme. The Board shall approve this policy and any
     material amendments, allocate adequate resources to the
     compliance function, receive periodic reports from the MLRO, and
     satisfy itself that the programme is effective.

3.2  Senior Management

     Senior Management is responsible for the day-to-day implementation
     of the controls described in this policy. Senior Management shall
     ensure that the MLRO has the authority, independence, and access
     to information needed to discharge the role, and shall act
     promptly on any recommendations or escalations from the MLRO.

3.3  Money Laundering Reporting Officer (the MLRO)

     The MLRO is the designated officer responsible for overseeing
     compliance with this policy and with all applicable AML and CFT
     laws and regulations. The MLRO shall:

     (a) Maintain and update the enterprise-wide risk assessment.
     (b) Oversee the customer due diligence programme.
     (c) Receive, investigate, and where appropriate file suspicious
         transaction reports and other mandatory reports with the FIU
         through goAML.
     (d) Administer targeted financial sanctions screening.
     (e) Deliver or arrange training and awareness programmes.
     (f) Report periodically to Senior Management and the Board on the
         state of the AML and CFT programme.
     (g) Act as the primary point of contact with the MOE and the FIU.

3.4  All Staff

     Every member of staff has a personal obligation to comply with
     this policy, to complete all required training, and to report
     any knowledge or suspicion of money laundering or terrorist
     financing to the MLRO promptly and confidentially.

4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  Standard Due Diligence (SDD)

     Before establishing a business relationship or carrying out an
     occasional transaction above the applicable threshold, the firm
     shall identify and verify the identity of the customer, any
     beneficial owner, and any person acting on behalf of the customer.
     The firm shall understand the purpose and intended nature of the
     business relationship and obtain sufficient information to build
     a risk profile.

4.2  Simplified Due Diligence

     Where the enterprise-wide risk assessment and the individual risk
     assessment of a customer demonstrate that the risk of money
     laundering or terrorist financing is low, the MLRO may authorise
     the application of simplified measures. The firm shall document
     the rationale and retain it on file. Simplified due diligence
     shall never be applied where there is a suspicion of money
     laundering or terrorist financing.

4.3  Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

     The firm shall apply enhanced due diligence measures where the
     risk is assessed as higher than normal. Circumstances requiring
     EDD include, but are not limited to, customers who are politically
     exposed persons, customers from or in high-risk jurisdictions,
     complex or unusual ownership structures, and any situation where
     the MLRO determines that the standard measures are not sufficient
     to manage the risk.

4.4  The firm shall not establish or continue a business relationship
     where it is unable to complete due diligence to the standard
     required by this policy and by applicable law. Where due diligence
     cannot be completed on an existing customer, the firm shall
     consider filing a suspicious transaction report and shall exit
     the relationship in an orderly manner.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. ONGOING MONITORING AND TRANSACTION MONITORING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1  The firm shall conduct ongoing monitoring of every business
     relationship to ensure that transactions are consistent with the
     firm's knowledge of the customer, the customer's business, the
     customer's risk profile, and, where applicable, the source of
     funds and source of wealth.

5.2  The firm shall maintain transaction monitoring arrangements that
     are proportionate to the nature, size, and complexity of the
     firm's business. These arrangements shall be capable of detecting
     transactions that are unusual, have no apparent lawful purpose,
     or are inconsistent with the customer's known profile.

5.3  Where ongoing monitoring or transaction monitoring identifies
     activity that gives rise to a suspicion, the matter shall be
     escalated to the MLRO immediately.

5.4  The MLRO shall review the firm's monitoring rules and thresholds
     at least annually, and more frequently where the risk environment
     changes, to ensure that they remain effective.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1  Any member of staff who knows, suspects, or has reasonable grounds
     to suspect that a transaction, attempted transaction, or funds
     are related to money laundering, the financing of terrorism, or
     the financing of illegal organisations shall report the matter
     to the MLRO without delay.

6.2  The MLRO shall assess every internal report and, where the
     suspicion is maintained after investigation, shall file the
     appropriate report with the FIU through the goAML platform. The
     report types include:

     (a) Suspicious Transaction Report (STR).
     (b) Suspicious Activity Report (SAR).
     (c) Disposal of Property related to Money Laundering, Suspicion
         Report (DPMSR).
     (d) Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons and their Means of Delivery
         Report (PNMR).
     (e) Funds Freezing Report (FFR).

6.3  The MLRO shall file the report with the FIU promptly and within
     any time limits prescribed by law or by the FIU. The firm shall
     not carry out or complete a transaction where the FIU has
     instructed a hold, except with the written consent of the FIU.

6.4  Tipping off is a criminal offence. No person shall disclose to
     the customer, or to any third party not authorised to receive
     the information, that a report has been filed or that an
     investigation is underway.

6.5  The MLRO shall maintain a confidential register of all internal
     disclosures received and all reports filed with the FIU,
     including the outcome and any feedback received.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. TARGETED FINANCIAL SANCTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1  The firm shall comply fully with all obligations arising from
     United Nations Security Council (UNSC) Resolutions relating to
     the freezing of assets, the prohibition on making funds or
     economic resources available to designated persons or entities,
     and the prohibition on facilitating travel.

7.2  The firm shall screen all customers, beneficial owners, connected
     parties, and counterparties against the following lists, at a
     minimum:

     (a) The UNSC Consolidated Sanctions List.
     (b) The UAE Local Terrorist List.
     (c) Any list or notice issued by the Executive Office of the
         Committee for Goods and Materials Subject to Import and
         Export Control (EOCN).

7.3  Screening shall be performed at the point of onboarding, upon
     any change in customer information, and on an ongoing basis when
     lists are updated. The MLRO shall ensure that list updates are
     applied without undue delay.

7.4  Where a match is confirmed, the firm shall freeze the relevant
     funds or assets immediately, without prior notice to the customer,
     and shall notify the EOCN and the FIU in accordance with the
     applicable procedures and time limits. The MLRO shall file an
     FFR through goAML where required.

7.5  The firm shall not take any action that would result in the
     release or transfer of frozen funds except with the written
     authorisation of the competent authority.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. PEP SCREENING AND ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

8.1  The firm shall maintain reasonable measures to determine whether
     a customer, a beneficial owner, or a person connected to the
     customer is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP), whether foreign,
     domestic, or holding a prominent position in an international
     organisation.

8.2  Where a PEP is identified, the firm shall, in addition to
     standard due diligence:

     (a) Obtain approval from the MLRO (or Senior Management, as the
         case may be) before establishing or continuing the business
         relationship.
     (b) Take reasonable measures to establish the source of wealth
         and the source of funds.
     (c) Conduct enhanced ongoing monitoring of the relationship.

8.3  PEP status does not, by itself, mean that a customer is involved
     in criminal activity. The enhanced measures are applied to manage
     the higher inherent risk associated with the position held.

8.4  The firm shall continue to treat a customer as a PEP for a
     period determined by the risk assessment after the person ceases
     to hold the relevant position, and in any event for not less than
     the period prescribed by applicable law or guidance.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. RECORD-KEEPING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

9.1  The firm shall retain all records obtained through customer due
     diligence, including identification documents, verification
     records, and the results of any analysis undertaken, for a period
     of not less than ten (10) years from the date on which the
     business relationship ended or the occasional transaction was
     carried out.

9.2  The firm shall retain all transaction records for a period of not
     less than ten (10) years from the date on which the transaction
     was completed.

9.3  Records relating to suspicious transaction reports, internal
     disclosures, and correspondence with the FIU shall be retained
     for a period of not less than ten (10) years from the date of
     the report or disclosure.

9.4  All records shall be maintained in a manner that allows them to
     be retrieved promptly and provided to the MOE, the FIU, or any
     other competent authority upon request.

9.5  The retention periods stated in this section are minimum periods.
     Where the MLRO considers it prudent, or where a matter is under
     investigation, records shall be retained for a longer period.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. TRAINING AND AWARENESS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

10.1 The firm shall provide AML and CFT training to all staff at the
     time of joining and at least annually thereafter. Training shall
     cover, at a minimum:

     (a) The legal and regulatory framework, including the obligations
         arising under Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025.
     (b) The firm's policies, procedures, and controls.
     (c) How to identify and report suspicious activity.
     (d) Targeted financial sanctions obligations.
     (e) The consequences of non-compliance for the individual and
         for the firm.

10.2 Staff in roles with direct customer contact or higher exposure
     to ML and TF risk shall receive additional, role-specific
     training as determined by the MLRO.

10.3 The MLRO shall maintain a training register recording the date
     of each session, the topics covered, the attendees, and the
     results of any assessment. This register shall be available for
     inspection by the MOE at any time.

10.4 The MLRO shall assess the effectiveness of the training
     programme at least annually and shall update the content to
     reflect changes in law, regulation, typologies, and the firm's
     own risk profile.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. INTERNAL CONTROLS AND COMPLIANCE TESTING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

11.1 The firm shall maintain internal controls that are proportionate
     to the nature, size, and risk profile of its business. These
     controls shall be documented in supporting procedures and shall
     cover, at a minimum, customer due diligence, transaction
     monitoring, sanctions screening, reporting, and record-keeping.

11.2 The MLRO shall conduct, or arrange for the conduct of, an
     independent compliance review at least annually to assess
     whether the controls set out in this policy and in the
     supporting procedures are operating effectively.

11.3 The findings of each compliance review shall be reported to
     Senior Management and to the Board, together with recommended
     remedial actions and target dates for completion.

11.4 Any deficiencies identified shall be remediated promptly. The
     MLRO shall track open items and report on progress at each
     subsequent review cycle.

11.5 The firm shall cooperate fully with any inspection, examination,
     or inquiry by the MOE or any other competent authority, and
     shall provide all requested documents and information within the
     time frames specified.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. POLICY REVIEW AND APPROVAL
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

12.1 This policy shall be reviewed by the MLRO at least once every
     twelve months, or sooner if there is a material change in the
     law, the regulatory environment, the firm's business model, or
     the firm's risk profile.

12.2 Any amendments to this policy shall be submitted to the Board
     for approval before they take effect.

12.3 Following each review, whether or not amendments are made, the
     MLRO shall record the date of the review, the outcome, and the
     rationale for any changes in the policy review log.

12.4 This policy and all amendments shall be communicated to every
     person to whom it is addressed within a reasonable time of
     approval.
