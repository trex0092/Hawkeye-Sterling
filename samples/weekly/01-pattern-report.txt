1. PURPOSE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the weekly pattern report prepared for the MLRO by the
compliance function. It is an internal analytical document. It is not
addressed to Senior Management and it is not part of the formal Weekly
MLRO Report to Senior Management, although it is used as a source for
that document. The pattern report is intended to give the MLRO a
weekly synthesis of cross-entity typology activity that is harder to
see in the daily cycle.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE WEEK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The dominant theme of the week is the recycled gold sanctions typology
recorded against three separate programme entities. It is the first
time this cycle that the same typology has appeared simultaneously on
three entities, and in our view the right response is to treat the
three files as one investigation track with a single lead reviewer.

High cash intensity activity is also rising, with nine separate high
cash intensity events recorded across four programmes in the week,
compared with four events across two programmes the previous week.
The compliance function is not yet calling this an attributable
pattern, but it is the kind of early warning we would expect to see
a week before a coordinated event in the DPMS sector.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CROSS-ENTITY PATTERNS OBSERVED
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pattern 1. Recycled gold from possibly linked counterparties.
Observed on FG BRANCH, ZOE FZE and MADISON LLC, each naming a
counterparty with a Ras Al Khaimah trade licence and a shared corporate
suffix. The compliance function has opened a shared counterparty file
in the cross-entity counterparty register and has recommended that a
single lead reviewer be assigned to the three files with effect from
next week.

Pattern 2. Rising high cash intensity.
Observed on FG BRANCH, FG LLC, MADISON LLC and NAPLES LLC. The rise is
most concentrated in NAPLES LLC, which recorded five of the nine events.
No single customer accounts for more than two events.

Pattern 3. CDD exemption claims above baseline.
Observed on FG BRANCH and MADISON LLC. The compliance function recorded
seven exemption claims across the two entities in the week, against a
baseline of three to four per week across the whole portfolio. We are
not yet concerned but we are tracking.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. EMERGING RISKS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two risks are categorised as emerging by the compliance function this
week.

First, the recycled gold sanctions exposure on the FG BRANCH, ZOE FZE
and MADISON LLC files. If the three counterparties are confirmed to be
related, the firm may be facing its first coordinated sanctions risk
event of the year. We recommend that the MLRO place this item at the
top of the agenda for the weekly compliance committee.

Second, the NAPLES LLC cash intensity signal. If next week shows the
same rate, the compliance function will recommend the MLRO open a
formal review of the NAPLES LLC programme's cash handling controls.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. STALLED INVESTIGATIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function reports three stalled investigations this week.
An investigation is treated as stalled if it has been open for more
than fourteen calendar days without a material movement in Asana.

FG BRANCH, RF-R17 Recycled Gold as Hawala Alternative. Open 18 days.
Blocked pending response from the branch manager. Two reminders sent.

FG LLC, RF-R14 Recycled Gold from Conflict or Looted Sources. Open 21
days. Blocked pending a third-party expert opinion on the material.
The expert is due to respond on 08 April 2026.

GRAMALTIN AS, RF-R20 Annual Mined and Recycled Red Flags Review.
Open 19 days. Blocked pending the annual management review of the
GRAMALTIN AS programme.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. WINS OF THE WEEK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function records the following material wins this week.

FG BRANCH closed four historical Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones
Report candidate reviews that had been carried on the books since the
beginning of the year. All four were filed through the Financial
Intelligence Unit on the goAML platform during the week with the MLRO's
approval.

ZOE FZE fully refreshed the Enhanced Due Diligence file on its two
existing PEP customers, bringing the programme back to a current
state on PEP file maintenance.

The firm completed its quarterly staff sanctions training on schedule.
All 23 staff members whose roles expose them to sanctions risk have
completed the module and are current on training records.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. RECOMMENDED FOCUS FOR THE WEEK AHEAD
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function recommends the following three focus items for
the week of 06 to 10 April 2026.

First, resolve the recycled gold sanctions track across FG BRANCH, ZOE
FZE and MADISON LLC. Assign a single lead reviewer. Prioritise the
collection of discriminating data on the counterparty identities.

Second, set up a review of NAPLES LLC cash handling controls and record
in the file the basis for whether a formal review is warranted at this
stage.

Third, resolve the three stalled investigations listed in section 5,
either by bringing them to a close or by recording an explicit reason
for continued inactivity.
