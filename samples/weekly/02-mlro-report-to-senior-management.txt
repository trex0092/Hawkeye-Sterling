1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the weekly report of the Money Laundering Reporting Officer to
Senior Management of HAWKEYE STERLING V2 for the week ending Friday
03 April 2026. It covers the firm's compliance with its obligations as
a Designated Non-Financial Business and Profession under the applicable
provisions of Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 on Anti-Money
Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism and Financing of
Illegal Organisations, as supervised by the Ministry of Economy in the
firm's capacity as a Dealer in Precious Metals and Stones.

This report is an internal document prepared in support of the
continuing duty of the firm to maintain a Money Laundering Reporting
Officer and to ensure Senior Management is informed of the operation
of the firm's AML and CFT programme. It is not a filing to any
supervisory authority. It is retained in the firm's archive for ten
years.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE OF THE WEEK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm carried three material risk items during the week and took
action on each of them. The most significant item was a suspected
sanctions nexus on the FG BRANCH recycled gold programme, which
triggered the suspension of a proposed settlement of AED 164,000 and
the opening of a Partial Name Match review that remains ongoing. The
programme is under control. There are no matters on which I, in my
capacity as MLRO, am unable to obtain the resources I need.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. FILINGS MADE THROUGH THE GOAML PLATFORM THIS WEEK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm filed, through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML
platform, the following reports during the week. Each filing was made
on the basis of my personal review of the underlying material and my
signature.

Suspicious Transaction Reports filed:              0
Suspicious Activity Reports filed:                 0
Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports:     4
Partial Name Match Reports filed:                  0
Funds Freeze Reports filed:                        0

All four Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports filed this week
arose from historical cash transactions carried on the FG BRANCH books
since the beginning of the year. The underlying cash thresholds were
confirmed against the applicable framework and the filings discharged
the firm's reporting obligation for those transactions.

The compliance function has additionally prepared, for my consideration,
one Suspicious Transaction Report candidate review and one Partial Name
Match Report candidate review. I will make a determination on both
during the first half of next week.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. MATTERS UNDER REVIEW BY THE MLRO AT WEEK-END
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following matters are under my personal review as at 17:00 on
Friday 03 April 2026.

a. FG BRANCH RF-R12, recycled gold from a counterparty that cannot be
   discharged against the UN Security Council Consolidated List on our
   own record. Status: settlement suspended, Partial Name Match review
   opened, awaiting collection of three specific discriminating data
   items by 06 April 2026.

b. ZOE FZE RF-R12 variant, recycled gold from a counterparty sharing a
   corporate suffix and a trade licence range with the FG BRANCH
   counterparty in item a. Status: transaction suspended, Partial Name
   Match Report candidate review prepared.

c. MADISON LLC RF-R05, aggregation of linked cash transactions from
   the same customer in the rolling 30 day window. Status: Dealers in
   Precious Metals and Stones Report candidate review prepared,
   awaiting my determination.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SANCTIONS SCREENING ACTIVITY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

During the week the firm performed 1,062 unique name queries against
the UN Security Council Consolidated List and the UAE Local Terrorist
List, as administered by the Executive Office for Control and
Non-Proliferation. The screening engine returned 11 hits. Nine of the
eleven hits were cleared as false positives on documented discriminating
data. Two remain under review, both relating to the recycled gold
sanctions track described in section 4 above. No funds have been
released to any counterparty while a match is under review.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PEP POPULATION AND ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's identified PEP population stands at 14 customers at the
close of the week. All 14 are under Enhanced Due Diligence. All 14
customer files carry my personal authorisation to continue the
relationship.

One new PEP was identified this week (FG BRANCH, customer reference
FGB-CUST-2026-00418). I am to consider the continuation of the
relationship and will record my decision on that file at the start of
next week. One customer moved out of the PEP population at the end of
her twelve-month cooling-off period (MADISON LLC, customer reference
MAD-CUST-2024-00092).

7. TRAINING AND COMPETENCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm completed its quarterly sanctions training module during the
week. All 23 staff members whose duties expose them to sanctions risk
have completed the module and are current on training records. No
exceptions to report.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. RISK ASSESSMENT AND PROGRAMME HEALTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

In my view, the firm's compliance with its obligations under Federal
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 during the week ending 03 April 2026 has
been satisfactory. The recycled gold sanctions track described in
section 4 is a material risk event but is being handled within the
firm's procedures. I have the resources and the authority I need to
discharge my function.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. RECOMMENDATIONS TO SENIOR MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I respectfully invite Senior Management to note the contents of this
report and, in particular, to note the following three points.

First, the recycled gold sanctions track is sufficiently material to
warrant an item on the agenda of the next standing meeting of Senior
Management. I will attend to present the state of the file and answer
any questions.

Second, the rise in high cash intensity activity on NAPLES LLC warrants
a forward look at the programme's cash handling controls. I intend to
open a formal review next week and will report in the next weekly
report on the outcome.

Third, the firm's compliance resources are adequate for current
activity but should be reviewed if the recycled gold sanctions track
escalates into a coordinated investigation across three entities. I
will flag this explicitly if it arises.
