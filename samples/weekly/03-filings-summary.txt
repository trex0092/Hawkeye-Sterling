-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. NOTABLE DRAFTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. STR draft, Entity Alpha, RF-R12. This draft was triggered by the
   recycled gold counterparty whose trade licence and registered office
   have not been reconciled with the available sanctions list data. The
   compliance function prepared this draft on the basis that, if the
   match is confirmed, the MLRO may wish to file a Suspicious Transaction
   Report rather than a Partial Name Match Report. The MLRO is invited to
   review the underlying evidence and determine the appropriate filing
   type.

2. DPMSR draft, Entity Echo, linked cash transactions. Three customers
   of the Entity Echo programme reached the firm's internal linked-
   transaction threshold in the rolling 30-day window during the week.
   The compliance function prepared a single consolidated Dealers in
   Precious Metals and Stones Report draft covering all three customers.
   The MLRO is invited to confirm whether a consolidated report or three
   individual reports is the correct approach for submission.

3. DPMSR draft, Entity Bravo, walk-in customer. A walk-in customer
   sold recycled gold for AED 195,000 in cash on 01 April 2026. The
   transaction was above the firm's internal reporting threshold and
   the compliance function prepared a routine Dealers in Precious Metals
   and Stones Report draft.

4. DPMSR draft, Entity Alpha, periodic aggregation. The rolling 30-day
   aggregation for one regular customer of the Entity Alpha programme
   crossed the firm's internal linked-transaction threshold on 02 April
   2026. A routine Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Report draft
   has been prepared.

5. PNMR draft, Entity Foxtrot, RF-R12. The counterparty on the Entity
   Foxtrot RF-R12 file produced a medium-confidence match against a
   name on the United Nations Security Council Consolidated List. The
   compliance function has prepared a Partial Name Match Report draft
   for the MLRO's review. The MLRO may wish to read this draft in
   conjunction with the STR draft noted at item 1 above, as both
   concern the same counterparty group.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. REMINDER ON FILING MODE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

All five filing types remain in manual mode. No draft produced by the
compliance function is filed automatically. Every draft is held for
the MLRO's personal review and, where the MLRO is satisfied that filing
is appropriate, submission is carried out by the MLRO through the goAML
platform using her own credentials. The compliance function will not
submit any filing without the MLRO's express instruction.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

   Suspicious Transaction Report or a Partial Name Match Report is the
