1. HEADLINE FOR TODAY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The dominant exposure across the portfolio today is a variant of the
recycled gold sanctions typology recorded on three separate programmes:
FG BRANCH, ZOE FZE and MADISON LLC. The three files name counterparties
with similar trading patterns and overlapping licence ranges. In the view
of the compliance function these items should be handled as a single
coordinated investigation track rather than as three independent reviews,
and a named reviewer should be assigned across the three entities before
the end of the day.

One item on this track, FG BRANCH RF-R12, is already two days overdue
and sits at the top of our risk score scale. The compliance function
recommends that the MLRO take a first decision on that file before 12:00
today.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE FIVE PORTFOLIO PRIORITIES FOR TODAY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Item 1. FG BRANCH, RF-R12 Recycled Gold from Sanctioned Entities.
The counterparty profile on this file touches the targeted financial
sanctions framework administered by the Executive Office for Control
and Non-Proliferation. The compliance function cannot discharge the
name match on its own record and recommends that a Partial Name Match
review be opened today. If the match is later confirmed, a Funds Freeze
Report will have to follow within the timeline set by the applicable
framework.

Item 2. ZOE FZE, RF-R12 counterparty variant, Al Safwa Metals Trading.
The counterparty on this file shares a corporate suffix and a Ras Al
Khaimah trade licence range with the FG BRANCH entity in item 1. The
phonetic similarity of the counterparty name to a list entry on the
UN Security Council Consolidated List is high enough to warrant a
Partial Name Match review. The transaction on this file has been
suspended at branch level pending clearance.

Item 3. MADISON LLC, RF-R05 Jewelry Scrap Aggregation Without CDD.
Three linked cash purchases from the same customer dated 11 March,
19 March and 02 April 2026 with an aggregate cash consideration of
AED 64,000 in the rolling 30 day window. A Dealers in Precious Metals
and Stones Report candidate review has been prepared for MLRO
consideration.

Item 4. FG BRANCH, RF-R08 Cross-Border Recycled Gold Smuggling.
A walk-in customer sold seven recycled gold bars for AED 280,000 in
cash on 02 April 2026 against documentation that does not reconcile
with the declared source of the material. A Suspicious Transaction
Report candidate review has been prepared and the underlying payment
to the customer is on hold pending the MLRO's decision.

Item 5. NAPLES LLC, NRA-RF01 High Cash Intensity Patterns.
Two new high cash intensity events were recorded against this
programme this week, taking the total for the month to four. The
pattern does not yet meet the compliance function's internal
escalation threshold, but it is the kind of shift that has preceded
coordinated typology events in our sector in previous cycles and the
compliance function is surfacing it on that basis.

3. CROSS-ENTITY PATTERNS OBSERVED TODAY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function observed two patterns across the portfolio today
that would not be visible from a single-entity review.

First, the RF-R12 typology now appears on the top ten of three separate
entities on the same day. The counterparty names are not identical, but
two of the three share a corporate suffix and a trade licence range in
Ras Al Khaimah. In our view the three counterparties may be related and
the three compliance files should be treated as one investigation track.
A shared counterparty file has been opened in the cross-entity
counterparty register.

Second, high cash intensity red flags under NRA-RF01 are recorded
against three entities this week, up from one the previous week. This
is not yet a pattern we can attribute with confidence, but it is the
kind of early warning that our weekly pattern report picks up a week
later and by then the exposure has grown. The compliance function
therefore prefers to surface it in today's digest rather than wait.
