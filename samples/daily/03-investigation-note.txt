1. PURPOSE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This note is a preparation note for the MLRO's review of the subject file
and is not itself a decision document. The compliance function has not
formed a suspicion against any identified individual at this stage. The
purpose of the note is to place in front of the MLRO the facts we have,
the facts we still need, the applicable regulatory hooks, and a proposed
line of inquiry, so that the MLRO can start the review without having to
reconstruct the file from scratch.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SUMMARY OF THE FILE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The subject file was opened on 27 March 2026 following a referral from
the FG Branch manager. The referral concerned a proposed settlement to
a counterparty trading under the name Al Safwa Metals Trading FZ LLC,
holding trade licence RAK-XXXXXX issued in Ras Al Khaimah. The proposed
settlement was for AED 164,000 for the purchase of recycled gold bars
delivered to the FG Branch office on 25 March 2026.

Our internal sanctions screening on 25 March 2026 returned a 91 percent
name match against a list entry on the UN Security Council Consolidated
List. The list entry is "Al Safwa Metals" with no legal form and no
country of registration attached. The compliance function cannot
discharge the match on its own record.

Since the match was raised, the settlement has been held. The physical
material is retained in the branch safe under dual control and has not
moved. The counterparty has not been informed of the match.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. FACTS HELD ON FILE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

We hold the following on the subject file at the time of preparation.

a. A copy of trade licence RAK-XXXXXX showing the legal name of the
   counterparty, its activity classification as a precious metals
   trading company, and the name of its sole shareholder of record,
   Mr. Yusuf Kareem Al Ashmawi.

b. A copy of the Emirates ID of the sole shareholder, with identification
   number 784-1976-XXXXXXX-X.

c. A copy of the delivery note dated 25 March 2026, describing seven
   recycled gold bars with a declared weight of 2,415 grams and a
   declared fineness of 999.

d. A photograph of the seven bars under dual control lighting taken at
   the time of delivery.

e. A weight certificate bearing the letterhead of a refiner registered
   outside the United Arab Emirates. The certificate identifies the
   refiner by a name that does not match our approved refiner list.

f. The internal sanctions screening report dated 25 March 2026, which
   returned the 91 percent match.

g. A walk-in visitor log for 25 March 2026 recording the attendance of
   an individual identifying himself as the shareholder of the
   counterparty. The log matches the Emirates ID at point b.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. FACTS WE DO NOT HOLD AND NEED
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function has identified five pieces of information whose
absence is currently preventing closure of the file. The MLRO may wish
to direct their collection.

a. A certified translation into Arabic or English of the refiner's
   weight certificate, together with verifiable contact details for the
   refiner itself.

b. A confirmed beneficial ownership declaration for the counterparty,
   signed and dated by the sole shareholder, with the prescribed
   supporting identification documents for any person holding twenty
   five percent or more of the counterparty or exercising ultimate
   control.

c. An independent source of wealth declaration for Mr. Yusuf Kareem
   Al Ashmawi, supported by at least one documentary source. His
   current Emirates ID does not evidence source of wealth.

d. The counterparty's audited financial statements for the year ended
   31 December 2025, or if unavailable, management accounts to the
   nearest completed quarter.

e. Any evidence, on its face independent of the counterparty, that
   confirms the identity of the list entry on the UN Security Council
   Consolidated List is distinct from the counterparty we are dealing
   with, or alternatively that confirms the two are the same.

Until items b, c and e are received, the compliance function is of the
view that the file cannot be discharged and the settlement should not
proceed.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. KEY CONTROL QUESTIONS A REGULATOR WOULD ASK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function has drawn up the following short list of control
questions. We expect these, or something close to them, to be asked by
the Ministry of Economy in the course of any supervisory review of this
file, and the MLRO may wish to answer them internally before closing the
file.

First, when was the sanctions match identified, how was it recorded, and
what immediate action did the firm take?

Second, who is the beneficial owner of the counterparty, and how is that
beneficial ownership evidenced to a standard a supervisor would accept?

Third, what is the documented source of the recycled gold material on
the delivery of 25 March 2026, and does the firm have independent
corroboration of that source?

Fourth, is the refiner named on the weight certificate a refiner our
programme has approved, and if not, why did the branch proceed to accept
the material at all?

Fifth, in the event the sanctions match is later confirmed, what is the
firm's plan to freeze the assets and file a Funds Freeze Report through
the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML platform, and within what
time?

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. POSSIBLE OUTCOMES OF THE REVIEW
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function sees four plausible outcomes for the review.

Outcome A. The sanctions match is cleared on the basis of additional
discriminating information received and the file is closed with no
further action. The settlement proceeds.

Outcome B. The sanctions match remains unconfirmed but the compliance
function remains unable to discharge it. A Partial Name Match Report is
filed through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML platform.
The settlement remains suspended.

Outcome C. The sanctions match is confirmed. A Funds Freeze Report is
filed through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML platform.
The underlying material and any related accounts are frozen in
accordance with the applicable targeted financial sanctions framework.
The relationship with the counterparty is terminated.

Outcome D. The sanctions match is cleared but independent grounds for
a Suspicious Transaction Report arise during the review. A Suspicious
Transaction Report is filed on those independent grounds.

7. PROPOSED NEXT STEP

Open the review today. Commission items b, c and e from the counterparty
