6. ANALYTICAL COMMENTARY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Entity Alpha programme carries 526 open tasks, of which 12 are now
past their due dates. The overdue concentration is in the recycled gold
red flag series and the national risk assessment series, which together
account for 10 of the 12 overdue items. This is consistent with the
pattern the compliance function has observed across the past three
business days: the recycled gold workstream is absorbing the available
review capacity and lower-priority items in the same series are falling
behind.

The sanctions exposure on the RF-R12 file remains the most significant
open item on this programme. The counterparty named in the file has a
Ras Al Khaimah trade licence and a registered office that the compliance
function has not been able to reconcile with the information held on the
United Nations Security Council Consolidated List. Until the MLRO
completes a first review of this file, the compliance function is unable
to determine whether a Partial Name Match Report or a Funds Freeze Report
is required.

The cash intensity indicator on the NRA-RF01 file has been present for
three consecutive business days. The compliance function recommends that
the MLRO consider whether a linked-transaction aggregation review is
appropriate on this file before the weekly cycle closes on Friday.
