1. PURPOSE OF THIS NOTE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This note sets out the ten open compliance items in the Entity Alpha programme
that, in the view of the compliance function, warrant the MLRO's attention
today. The selection is made from the 526 open tasks currently held against
this programme and is intended to give the MLRO a single page to read before
the morning escalation call at 09:30.

The ranking is not a substitute for the MLRO's judgement. It is a first cut
prepared by the compliance function against the criteria set out in our
internal prioritisation procedure. Items one to three are items on which we
ask for a decision today.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. ITEMS AT RISK TODAY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Four items on this programme are either overdue or fall due within the next
three business days.

a. RF-R12, Recycled Gold from Sanctioned Entities. The item was due on
   03 April 2026 and is therefore two days overdue. The counterparty profile

b. RF-R08, Cross-Border Recycled Gold Smuggling. Due tomorrow, 06 April 2026.

3. TOP TEN ITEMS FOR TODAY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The risk score below is scored on a scale of zero to one hundred by the
compliance function against the criteria set out in our prioritisation
procedure. A score of ninety or above is treated as an item requiring
action within one business day.

Item 1. RF-R12 Recycled Gold from Sanctioned Entities. Risk score 94.
The file sits on a counterparty whose trade licence and registered office
do not fully reconcile with a list entry we cannot discharge on our own
record. The evidence available to us at present is consistent with a
Partial Name Match review and, potentially, with a Funds Freeze Report if
the match is confirmed. We recommend that the MLRO take a first decision
on this item before 12:00 today.
Task link: https://app.asana.com/0/1213645083721316/1155285207862107

Item 2. RF-R08 Cross-Border Recycled Gold Smuggling. Risk score 89.
A walk-in customer sold seven recycled gold bars for AED 280,000 in cash
on 02 April 2026. The weight certificate presented bears the letterhead
of a refiner outside the United Arab Emirates and the declared source of
the material is inconsistent with the certificate. The compliance function
has placed an internal hold on the transaction. A Suspicious Transaction
Report candidate review has been prepared and is awaiting the MLRO's
decision to file through the Financial Intelligence Unit on goAML.

Item 3. NRA-RF04 FIU Typology 1 TBML in Gold Trade. Risk score 83.
Two invoices on this file do not reconcile with the customs declarations
our freight partner has provided for the corresponding consignments. The
discrepancy is small in isolation but forms part of a pattern the
compliance function recorded against the same counterparty in February
2026. We recommend that the MLRO assign a named reviewer to this file
today.

Item 4. RF-R05 Jewelry Scrap Aggregation Without CDD. Risk score 78.
Three linked cash purchases from the same customer, dated 11, 19 March
and 02 April 2026, with an aggregate consideration of AED 64,000 in the
rolling 30 day window. The customer has a full CDD file but has not yet
been placed under Enhanced Due Diligence. The compliance function has
prepared a Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Report candidate review
and asks the MLRO to confirm the filing threshold.

Item 5. RF-R16 Old Gold and Inherited Gold Verification. Risk score 64.
The file contains a customer declaration but no corroborating evidence
of prior ownership. The material value is modest and the risk is
bounded, but closing the file without additional documentation would
leave a gap our internal audit function would flag. We recommend that
the branch manager request two additional documents before close.

Item 6. RF-R09 Recycled Material CDD Exemption Abuse. Risk score 61.
Three exemptions were claimed against our CDD procedure in the past
seven days. Two of the three do not meet the stated exemption criteria
on a closer reading. The compliance function recommends that the branch
manager reverse both exemptions and reopen the customer files.

Item 7. RF-R11 Recycled Gold Purity and Assay Manipulation. Risk score 55.
The assay results on two recent intakes differ from the declared
composition by more than three percentage points. The difference is
within our internal tolerance band but sits at the upper end. We
recommend a secondary assay on both batches before release.

Item 8. RF-R06 Industrial Scrap Gold Processing. Risk score 49.
Routine monitoring. The file will close on receipt of the pending
source-of-funds evidence from the counterparty, which is expected this
week. No action today.

Item 9. RF-R10 Gold for Cash Schemes via Recycled Material. Risk score 44.
Routine monitoring. No new red flags in the past seven days. This file
will be reviewed again at the next weekly cycle.

Item 10. RF-R17 Recycled Gold as Hawala Alternative. Risk score 41.
Two items flagged last week are still awaiting clarification from the
FG Branch manager. The compliance function has sent a reminder and will
escalate if no response is received by close of business today.
