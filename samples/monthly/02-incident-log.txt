1. PURPOSE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the monthly incident log prepared by the compliance function
for the month of March 2026. It records every instance in which the
firm refused a transaction, declined an onboarding, identified a
compliance exception, or recorded a corrective action during the
month. The log is maintained as a permanent record and is retained
for a minimum of 10 years.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE MONTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function recorded 5 incidents during March 2026. Two
were refused transactions, one was a declined onboarding, and two were
compliance exceptions identified during routine review. None of the
incidents resulted in a regulatory notification beyond the filing
activity already recorded in the monthly MLRO consolidation report.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. INCIDENT ENTRIES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

a. 04 March 2026. Entity Delta. Category: refused transaction.
   A walk-in customer presented 12 recycled gold bars for sale,
   total declared weight 3.6 kilograms, with a declared value of
   approximately AED 720,000. The customer was unable to produce a
   valid source-of-material certificate or a purchase invoice for
   the material. The branch compliance lead at Entity Delta refused
   the transaction at the point of sale. The customer was informed
   of the reason for the refusal. No payment was made and no gold
   changed hands. The compliance function recorded the refusal in
   the transaction refusal register and notified the MLRO on the
   same day. The MLRO determined that no filing was required on the
   basis that the transaction did not complete.

b. 11 March 2026. Entity Alpha. Category: declined onboarding.
   A corporate customer applied to open a trading account with
   Entity Alpha for the purpose of purchasing bullion for export.
   During the Customer Due Diligence process the compliance function
   identified that the beneficial owner of the applicant company is
   resident in a jurisdiction that the firm classifies as high risk.
   The applicant was unable to provide satisfactory evidence of the
   source of funds for the proposed initial purchase. The compliance
   function recommended that the onboarding be declined. The MLRO
   reviewed the file and approved the decline on 12 March 2026.
   The applicant was informed by letter on 13 March 2026.

c. 17 March 2026. Entity Echo. Category: compliance exception.
   During a routine review of the Entity Echo cash transaction log,
   the compliance function identified that a linked-transaction
   aggregation for one customer had crossed the firm's internal
   reporting threshold on 14 March 2026 but that the Dealers in
   Precious Metals and Stones Report draft was not prepared until
   17 March 2026, a delay of three business days against the firm's
   internal target of one business day. The compliance function
   recorded the exception, prepared the draft, and notified the MLRO.
   The MLRO reviewed and filed the report on 18 March 2026. No
   regulatory deadline was missed. Corrective action: the compliance
   function has added an automated alert to the Entity Echo cash
   monitoring workflow to prevent recurrence.

d. 22 March 2026. Entity Bravo. Category: refused transaction.
   A regular customer of Entity Bravo attempted to sell old gold
   jewellery for AED 48,000 in cash. During the transaction the
   branch identified that the customer's identification document
   on file had expired. The transaction was paused and the customer
   was asked to present a valid identification document. The customer
   left the branch without completing the transaction and without
   presenting updated identification. The compliance function
   recorded the refusal and updated the customer file. The MLRO
   was notified on the same day. No filing was considered necessary.

e. 28 March 2026. Entity Alpha. Category: compliance exception.
   The compliance function identified that a PEP-tagged customer
   file on the Entity Alpha programme had not been reviewed within
   the firm's Enhanced Due Diligence refresh cycle. The file was
   due for review on 15 March 2026 and was 13 days overdue at the
   point of detection. The compliance function escalated the file
   to the MLRO. The MLRO completed the EDD review on 30 March 2026.
   Corrective action: the compliance function has added the file to
   the weekly dormant file review reminder to prevent recurrence.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. TRENDS AND PATTERNS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function does not identify a cross-incident pattern in
the five incidents recorded during March 2026. The two refused
transactions occurred at different branches and involved different
customers with different fact patterns. The two compliance exceptions
are procedural in nature and have been addressed through corrective
actions.

The compliance function notes that the frequency of incidents in March
2026 (five) is slightly above the baseline of three to four per month
observed in the previous quarter. The increase is attributable to the
two compliance exceptions, which reflect the compliance function's
improved detection capability rather than a deterioration in the firm's
control environment.
