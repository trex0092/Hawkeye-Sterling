1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the monthly consolidation report of the Money Laundering
Reporting Officer of HAWKEYE STERLING V2 for the month of March 2026.
It is prepared for Senior Management and for the Board of HAWKEYE
STERLING V2 in support of the continuing duty of the firm to maintain
a Money Laundering Reporting Officer and to ensure the governing body
is informed of the operation of the firm's AML and CFT programme. It
is an internal report. It has not been filed with any supervisory
authority. It is retained in the firm's archive for 10 years.

The report consolidates the four weekly MLRO reports issued during the
month and adds a monthly trend narrative, a filings summary, and a
set of recommendations to Senior Management.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE MONTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's compliance programme in March 2026 was dominated by two
themes. The first was the emergence of a recycled gold sanctions
typology that now touches three separate programme entities. That
exposure is under active management and is expected to result in at
least one regulator-facing filing during April 2026. The second was
a modest rise in high cash intensity activity on the NAPLES LLC
programme, which I consider to be an early warning rather than an
established pattern and which I intend to address through a formal
review of cash handling controls in the first half of April 2026.

There are no matters on which I, in my capacity as MLRO, have been
unable to obtain the resources or the authority I need to discharge
my function during March 2026.

5. SANCTIONS SCREENING AND TARGETED FINANCIAL SANCTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

During March 2026 the firm performed 4,821 unique name queries
against the UN Security Council Consolidated List and the UAE Local
Terrorist List, as administered by the Executive Office for Control
and Non-Proliferation. The screening engine returned 47 hits during
the month. Forty-three of the forty-seven hits were cleared as false
positives on documented discriminating data. Four remain under
review at month-end, and all four are connected to the recycled
gold sanctions track described in section 2 above. No funds have
been released to any counterparty while a match is under review at
any point during the month.

8. INTERNAL POLICY AND PROCEDURE CHANGES DURING THE MONTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's internal procedure on linked cash transaction aggregation
was updated on 12 March 2026 to reflect the rolling 30 day window
methodology. The previous procedure applied a calendar month window,
which could in theory allow a customer to structure across two
adjacent calendar months. The change was approved by Senior
Management on the same day. The new procedure is now in force at
all six programme branches.

No other internal policy or procedure changes were made during the
month.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. INCIDENTS AND EXCEPTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two incidents are reportable for the month.

On 05 March 2026 a branch cashier at MADISON LLC accepted a cash
transaction that ought to have been escalated to the branch compliance
officer under the revised linked transaction aggregation procedure.
The transaction was reviewed on the day and was ultimately not a
linked transaction on the correct reading, but the escalation should
still have occurred. The cashier was informed of the expected process
in writing. The relevant compliance training module has been refreshed.

On 18 March 2026 the firm's sanctions screening engine failed to
synchronise the UN Security Council Consolidated List at the 08:00
cycle. The failure was detected by the compliance function at 08:22.
The list was synchronised manually at 08:31. No customer queries
ran against a stale list at any point during the gap. The vendor of
the screening engine has been contacted and has issued a patch,
which has been applied.

11. RECOMMENDATIONS TO SENIOR MANAGEMENT AND THE BOARD
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I respectfully invite Senior Management and the Board to note the
contents of this report and, in particular, to consider the following
four items.

First, the recycled gold sanctions track across FG BRANCH, ZOE FZE
and MADISON LLC is material enough to warrant an agenda item at the
next Board meeting. I will attend to present the state of the files
and answer any questions.

Second, the rise in high cash intensity activity on NAPLES LLC
warrants a forward look at the programme's cash handling controls. I
intend to open a formal review during April 2026 and will report in
the April monthly report on the outcome.

Third, the firm should plan to refresh its enterprise-wide AML and
CFT risk assessment during the second quarter of 2026. I will prepare
a draft for Senior Management review by the end of May 2026.

Fourth, the compliance resources of the firm are adequate for current
activity but should be reviewed if the recycled gold sanctions track
escalates into a coordinated investigation across three entities. I
will flag this explicitly if and when it arises.
