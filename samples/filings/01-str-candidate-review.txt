5. GROUNDS FOR SUSPICION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function has reviewed the facts set out at section 4
above and the evidentiary material held on the Entity Alpha RF-R08 file.
Three independent grounds for suspicion arise from that review, and
each is stated below on the understanding that the MLRO will form her
own view on whether any, or all three, of them support a filing.

First, the documentation presented at the point of the 02 April 2026
transaction is internally inconsistent in a way the subject person has
not explained. The subject person stated that the material had been
acquired through a workshop in Ajman. The weight certificate handed
over at the counter bears the letterhead of a refiner outside the
United Arab Emirates and the dates on the two documents do not
reconcile. Copies of both documents are held on the file.

Second, the pattern of two cash transactions from the same subject
person in the rolling 60 day window, for a combined cash consideration
of AED 372,000, is consistent with a typology our programme tracks
for possible layering of precious metals through small-cap dealers.
The compliance function does not know whether any third party is
behind the subject person's activity, but the cadence of the two
transactions is not consistent with an occasional walk-in sale of
inherited or workshop material.

Third, the geographic origin implied by the refiner letterhead falls
within a jurisdiction that the compliance function classifies as
higher risk for conflict and recycled gold sourcing. The compliance
function is not in a position to confirm any specific sanctions
exposure from its own record. That limitation is stated transparently
here so the MLRO can decide whether to file on the other two grounds
alone or to wait for further information on the jurisdiction question.

Each of the three grounds is, in the view of the compliance function,
independently sufficient to meet the standard for filing. The
compliance function leaves to the MLRO the judgement on whether to
rely on one ground or on all three in the filed report.

6. ACTIONS TAKEN TO DATE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediately on identifying the inconsistencies at the counter the
compliance function placed an internal hold on the completion of the
transaction. Payment to the subject person has not been released. The
physical material is retained in the branch safe under dual control
and will not move until the MLRO has taken a decision. Both documents
presented at the counter have been photocopied and placed on the file.
A photograph of the seven bars under dual control lighting has been
taken and placed on the file. The subject person has been informed
that the matter is under internal review in the ordinary course of
our procedures and has not been informed of any suspicion or of any
intention to report.

No funds have moved. No material has moved. The subject person has
not been served with any notice.
