4. ACTIVITY THE SUBJECT OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

On 01 April 2026 the subject person attended the Entity Bravo branch at
Sharjah with a quantity of mixed karat jewellery scrap of approximate
total weight 1,180 grams. He presented himself for immediate cash
settlement on a walk-in basis and declined to open a customer file,
citing that the material belonged to a relative.

The branch compliance officer explained that the firm's standard
Customer Due Diligence procedure applies to any counterparty
presenting material for sale above our internal threshold and invited
the subject person to complete a short customer file.

On being told that Customer Due Diligence was required, the subject
person withdrew the request for cash settlement, took back the
material and left the branch without transacting. Before leaving he
asked the branch compliance officer whether there was any
"alternative arrangement" the firm could offer that would not require
a customer file. The branch compliance officer declined.

Twenty-two minutes later the same subject person returned to the
branch with a different individual, who presented himself as the
owner of the material. The second individual also declined to open
a customer file. Both individuals then left together without
transacting.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. GROUNDS FOR SUSPICION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function forms the view that three features of the
activity described above, taken together, give rise to a suspicion
that warrants reporting.

First, the request for an "alternative arrangement" that would avoid
the firm's Customer Due Diligence procedure is, in our reading, an
explicit attempt by the subject person to obtain the proceeds of a
sale without leaving an identifying record. This is a feature common
to the layering stage of money laundering typologies our programme
monitors.

Second, the return of the same individual with a second individual
claiming ownership of the material, without any documentary support
for the change of ownership, is consistent with a common tactic used
to defeat customer identification controls at the point of sale.

Third, the refusal to complete Customer Due Diligence on being asked
is consistent with an unwillingness to be identified, which is itself
a feature our typology list flags.

The compliance function does not draw any conclusion about the
lawful or unlawful origin of the material. The report is concerned
with the activity, not with a determination of its underlying cause.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. ACTIONS TAKEN TO DATE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The branch compliance officer recorded the attempted transaction in
the branch visitor log at 10:48 on 01 April 2026 and again at 11:10
on the same day. Both entries include a description of the material
and of the individuals. The branch CCTV footage covering the two
visits has been preserved and is held on file.

The subject person and the second individual are now on an internal
watch list. If either returns to any branch of [Reporting Entity],
the branch compliance officer has instructions to inform the
compliance function immediately and to decline any cash settlement
without a completed customer file.
