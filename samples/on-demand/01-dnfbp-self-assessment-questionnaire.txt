1. STATUS OF THIS DOCUMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a draft of the DNFBP self-assessment questionnaire that the
firm, in its capacity as a Dealer in Precious Metals and Stones, is
required to complete periodically for its supervisory authority, the
Ministry of Economy. The draft has been pre-filled by the compliance
function using the current state of the firm's AML and CFT programme,
the counterparty register, the training records and the recent filing
activity. It is not a filed document. It has no standing until the
MLRO has reviewed and corrected every answer, signed the declaration
at the end, and submitted the completed questionnaire through the
supervisory portal in her own name.

The field wording below is generic because the compliance function
does not have the current Ministry of Economy questionnaire template
verbatim. On receipt of the actual template the compliance function
will map each pre-filled answer below onto the corresponding field in
the official form, and the MLRO will validate the mapping before
submission.

3. GOVERNANCE AND AML FUNCTION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm has a designated Money Laundering Reporting Officer appointed
by the Board, with direct reporting to Senior Management and with
unrestricted access to the books and records of the firm. The MLRO
is supported by a compliance function that operates across all six
programme branches.

The Board reviews and approves the firm's AML and CFT policy and
procedures at least annually. The last Board approval was on [to be
inserted].

Senior Management receives a weekly report from the MLRO and a
monthly consolidation report. The MLRO attends Board meetings on
standing invitation when AML and CFT items are on the agenda.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE PROGRAMME
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm applies Customer Due Diligence at the opening of every
customer relationship, at the point of any occasional transaction
above the firm's internal threshold, and at periodic refresh intervals
set by the customer risk rating. The firm applies Enhanced Due
Diligence to all Politically Exposed Persons, to all customers on the
firm's internal high-risk pathway, and to any customer on whose file
the compliance function has formed a view that EDD is appropriate.

The firm's current identified PEP population is 14 customers. All 14
are under Enhanced Due Diligence. All 14 customer files carry explicit
MLRO authorisation to continue the relationship.

The firm's Customer Due Diligence procedure was last updated on 12
March 2026 in respect of linked cash transaction aggregation.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. TARGETED FINANCIAL SANCTIONS PROGRAMME
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm performs daily screening against the UN Security Council
Consolidated List and the UAE Local Terrorist List, as administered
by the Executive Office for Control and Non-Proliferation, on its
full active customer population and on every counterparty at
transaction time.

During the most recent monthly reporting period the firm performed
4,821 unique name queries and returned 47 hits, of which 43 were
cleared as false positives on documented discriminating data and 4
remain under review on the recycled gold sanctions track described
in the weekly MLRO report.

The firm has filed 1 Partial Name Match Report during the most
recent monthly reporting period and has no confirmed matches as at
the date of this draft.

7. RECORD KEEPING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm retains all Customer Due Diligence records, transaction
records, sanctions screening logs, training records and reporting
files for a minimum period of 10 years in accordance with the
applicable provision of Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025. The
firm's archive is held in electronic form in a version-controlled
repository and in hard copy at head office where hard copies exist.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. TRAINING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm delivered four scheduled training modules and two refresher
sessions during 2025, covering a total of 41 unique members of staff.
All staff members whose duties expose them to Money Laundering or
Terrorism Financing risk completed the modules required for their
role by the end of 2025. The training records and the attendance
sheets are retained in the firm's archive for 10 years.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. RISK ASSESSMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm maintains an annual enterprise-wide AML and CFT risk
assessment covering customer risk, product and service risk,
geographic risk, delivery channel risk and typology exposure. The
assessment for the year 2025 is currently in draft and is expected
to be signed off by the MLRO and presented to Senior Management and
the Board during the second quarter of 2026.

The firm assesses its residual AML and CFT risk for 2025 as medium
to high, principally driven by the recycled gold and jewellery
scrap business lines.

10. INCIDENTS AND EXCEPTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm recorded two reportable incidents during March 2026:

a. On 05 March 2026 a branch cashier at Entity Delta accepted a cash
   transaction that ought to have been escalated under the revised
   linked transaction aggregation procedure. The transaction was
   reviewed on the day and was ultimately not a linked transaction
   on the correct reading. The cashier was informed of the expected
   process in writing.

b. On 18 March 2026 the firm's sanctions screening engine failed to
   synchronise the UN Security Council Consolidated List at the
   08:00 cycle. The failure was detected at 08:22 and resolved at
   08:31. No customer query ran against a stale list during the gap.
