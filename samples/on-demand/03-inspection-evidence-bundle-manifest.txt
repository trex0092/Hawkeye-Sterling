1. PURPOSE OF THIS MANIFEST
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This manifest is the index to the Inspection Evidence Bundle prepared
by the compliance function for the 12 months ending 31 March 2026.
The bundle itself is a complete copy of every artefact produced by
the firm's compliance automation during that period, together with
the supporting files held on the compliance records. It is intended
to be produced, in full, to the Ministry of Economy or to the
Executive Office for Control and Non-Proliferation on demand during
a supervisory inspection, so that the firm's evidence trail is
immediately accessible and verifiable.

The bundle is held in electronic form in the repository and can also
be exported to a portable storage device for presentation in person.
Every file in the bundle is version-controlled and every change is
attributable to a commit in the repository history.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. BUNDLE CONTENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Folder: mlro-weekly/
52 Weekly MLRO Reports to Senior Management, one per week from the
week ending 05 April 2025 to the week ending 27 March 2026 inclusive.
Each report is signed by the MLRO and was acknowledged by Senior
Management on the business day following issue.

Folder: mlro-monthly/
12 Monthly MLRO Consolidation Reports, one per month from April 2025
to March 2026 inclusive. Each report is signed by the MLRO and
includes the Board minute reference for the subsequent Board meeting
at which the report was noted.

Folder: weekly-pattern/
52 Weekly Pattern Reports, the internal analytical companions to the
Weekly MLRO Reports, covering the same weeks.

Folder: daily/
252 daily folders, one per working day, each containing:
 - the per-project compliance priorities for each of the six active
   programmes,
 - the daily portfolio digest,
 - the daily sanctions screening log,
 - the daily PEP watch log,
 - any investigation preparation notes issued that day.

Folder: retro/
252 daily completion retros, one per working day, recording the hit
rate against the morning priority lists and the carry-over items.

Folder: filings/
A chronological record of every filing candidate review prepared by
the compliance function during the 12 month period, whether filed
or not, together with the goAML submission reference for each
actual filing. The folder contains:
 - 23 Suspicious Transaction Report candidate reviews, of which 17
   were filed through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML
   platform;
 - 7 Suspicious Activity Report candidate reviews, of which 5 were
   filed;
 - 149 Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Report candidate
   reviews, of which 144 were filed;
 - 6 Partial Name Match Report candidate reviews, of which 4 were
   filed;
 - 0 Funds Freeze Report candidate reviews filed. 1 draft prepared
   and held conditional on confirmation of a match that was
   subsequently cleared.

Folder: registers/
The cross-entity counterparty register as a CSV file, with a full
change history held in the repository. The current version records
415 counterparty entries covering all six programmes.

Folder: training/
The annual training completion report for 2025 and the quarterly
sanctions training records for 2025. Attendance sheets for each
session, competence assessment results for each attendee, and copies
of the training material as delivered on the day.

Folder: risk-assessment/
The annual enterprise-wide AML and CFT risk assessment for 2025 in
its signed form, and the draft for 2026 with change markings
showing the updates since 2025.

Folder: incidents/
A log of all compliance incidents recorded during the 12 month
period, with the corrective action taken and the date closed.

Folder: policies/
The current version of every internal AML and CFT policy, procedure
and control document in force during the period, with a change log
showing when each document was last updated and by whom.

3. HOW TO READ THE BUNDLE DURING AN INSPECTION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

For an inspector reading this bundle for the first time the
compliance function suggests the following short order of reading.

Read first, in order: the most recent Monthly MLRO Consolidation
Report, the most recent Weekly MLRO Report to Senior Management, and
the annual enterprise-wide risk assessment for 2025. Together these
three documents give the inspector a current picture of the firm's
programme from the top.

Read second: any filing candidate review of interest, together with
the underlying customer file referenced in it. The bundle supports
this cross-reference by document reference, Asana task GID and
customer reference.

Read third: the daily sanctions screening log for the period covered
by the inspection window, and the cross-entity counterparty register,
for evidence that the firm operates its targeted financial sanctions
programme on a continuing basis.

Read fourth: the training completion report and the attendance
sheets, for evidence that the firm meets its annual training
obligation.

Read fifth, only if the inspection is specifically interested in a
named customer or counterparty: the relevant entries in the
counterparty register and the corresponding filing candidate reviews.
