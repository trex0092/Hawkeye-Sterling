1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS PACK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This pack is prepared for the standing Board meeting scheduled for
15 April 2026 in discharge of my continuing obligation as Money
Laundering Reporting Officer to keep the Board informed of the
operation of the firm's AML and CFT programme. It is intended to be
read ahead of the meeting by every Board member and to serve as the
basis for any AML and CFT item on the agenda.

It consolidates the first two weekly reports of April 2026, the
most recent monthly consolidation for March 2026, the current state
of the recycled gold sanctions track, and a small set of forward-
looking recommendations that require Board attention.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. KEY MESSAGE FOR THE BOARD
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm is carrying one materially elevated AML and CFT exposure
into April 2026. That exposure is the recycled gold sanctions track
on Entity Alpha, Entity Foxtrot and Entity Delta. The track is under active
management. The MLRO considers that the firm's control framework is
adequate for the current state of the track, but the Board should
be aware that, if the match on the Entity Alpha counterparty is later
confirmed, the firm will be required to file a Funds Freeze Report
and to freeze the underlying material and proposed settlement. The
MLRO invites the Board to note this and to endorse the approach.

There are no matters on which the MLRO has been unable to obtain
the resources or authority she needs to discharge her function as
at the date of this pack.

4. SANCTIONS EXPOSURE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

At the date of this pack the firm holds four open sanctions reviews,
all of them connected to the recycled gold sanctions track.

The principal file is the Entity Alpha RF-R12 counterparty, on which
the compliance function has been unable to discharge a 91 percent
match against the UN Security Council Consolidated List on its own
record. The underlying settlement of AED 164,000 is suspended and
the physical material is retained in the branch safe under dual
control. A Partial Name Match Report has been filed. A Funds Freeze
Report candidate review has been prepared in draft, conditional on
confirmation of the match.

The MLRO invites the Board to note the exposure and to endorse the
following approach:

a. Hold the settlement and the material under dual control until
   the match is cleared or confirmed.
b. Escalate to a Funds Freeze Report within the applicable timeline
   if the match is confirmed.
c. Treat the Entity Alpha, Entity Foxtrot and Entity Delta files as a single
   cross-entity investigation track with a single lead reviewer.
