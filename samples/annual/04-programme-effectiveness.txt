1. PURPOSE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the annual programme effectiveness self-assessment for the
calendar year 2025. It scores the firm's AML and CFT programme against
five operational pillars, using a deterministic method based on the
count and completeness of artefacts produced by the compliance function
during the year. Each pillar is scored out of 20, giving a maximum
total of 100.

This assessment does not rely on narrative judgement. It is a factual
count of what the compliance function produced and archived. The MLRO
is invited to review the scores and the evidence cited for each pillar,
and to challenge any score where her professional judgement differs from
the arithmetic.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. OVERALL SCORE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total score:          82 out of 100
Rating:               SATISFACTORY

The rating scale used by the compliance function is:

  90 to 100           Strong
  75 to 89            Satisfactory
  60 to 74            Adequate
  40 to 59            Weak
  Below 40            Unsatisfactory

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. INTERPRETATION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The overall score of 82 places the firm's programme in the satisfactory
range. The strongest pillar is governance and MLRO reporting, which
reflects the consistent production of monthly and quarterly reports
throughout the year. The weakest pillar is filing draft preparation,
where three instances of delayed drafts reduced the score. The
compliance function has already addressed this gap through the
automated linked-transaction aggregation alert introduced in Q2 2025.

No pillar scored below 15. This indicates that the programme is
operating consistently across all five dimensions. The compliance
function does not recommend any structural change to the programme
for 2026.

d. Determine whether the overall score of 82 should be communicated
