1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the annual report of the Money Laundering Reporting Officer
of [Reporting Entity] for the calendar year 2025. It is prepared for
Senior Management and for the Board in discharge of my obligation to
report on the operation of the firm's AML and CFT programme at least
annually. It is an internal report. It has not been filed with any
supervisory authority. It is retained in the firm's archive for 10 years.

This report consolidates the four quarterly MLRO reports issued during
the year and adds an annual narrative, a filings summary, and a set of
recommendations for the coming year.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's AML and CFT programme operated at a satisfactory level
throughout 2025. Filing throughput was consistent across all four
quarters. The compliance function identified and escalated risks in
a timely manner, and no regulatory action was taken against the firm
during the year. One supervisory visit was conducted by the Ministry
of Economy in September 2025, which did not result in any finding or
remedial order.

The most significant compliance event of the year was the recycled
gold sanctions typology that emerged in the fourth quarter across
three programme entities. That matter was resolved by year-end with
the filing of one Suspicious Transaction Report and two Partial Name
Match Reports.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. ANNUAL FILINGS SUMMARY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm filed, through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML
platform, the following reports during the calendar year 2025:

Suspicious Transaction Reports filed:             12
Suspicious Activity Reports filed:                 6
Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports:   118
Partial Name Match Reports filed:                  5
Funds Freeze Reports filed:                        1
                                                  ---
Total filings through goAML during 2025:         142

The total of 142 filings represents an increase of 11 per cent over
the 128 filings made during 2024. The increase is attributable to the
rise in Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports, which in turn
reflects increased trading volume on the Entity Echo and Entity Bravo
programmes during the second half of the year.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. OPEN MATTERS AT YEAR-END
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

a. The recycled gold sanctions typology on three entities was resolved
   before year-end. No residual exposure remains open from this matter.

b. Two PEP files required Enhanced Due Diligence refresh during January
   2026. Both were on schedule at year-end.

c. One counterparty (Precious Refining AG, Switzerland) remains under
   enhanced monitoring following a transaction pattern identified in
   the third quarter. No filing has been considered necessary.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SANCTIONS SCREENING PROGRAMME FOR THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm screened its counterparty register and active customer
population on each business day of 2025 against the United Nations
Security Council Consolidated List and the UAE Local Terrorist List.
The total number of screening events during the year was approximately
62,400 (87 counterparties multiplied by approximately 250 business days,
plus ad hoc screenings of new relationships).

Confirmed matches:                0
Matches resolved as false positive: 11
Matches under review at year-end:   0

One Funds Freeze Report was filed in Q4 2025 in connection with a
counterparty that was added to the UAE Local Terrorist List during
the quarter. The relationship was terminated immediately.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PEP POPULATION MOVEMENT DURING THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

PEP files at 01 January 2025:    12
New PEP designations during year:  3
PEP files closed during year:      1
PEP files at 31 December 2025:   14

All 14 PEP files were under Enhanced Due Diligence at year-end. The
compliance function confirms that all EDD reviews were completed
within the firm's refresh cycle during the year, with the exception
of one file that was two days late in Q3 2025 and was addressed
through a corrective action.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. TRAINING PROGRAMME FOR THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm delivered the following AML and CFT training during 2025:

a. Quarterly AML refresher training across all six programmes. Four
   cycles completed. Completion rate: 97 per cent across all staff.

b. Annual sanctions awareness module. Completed by all staff across
   all six programmes.

c. Three new joiner inductions covering AML and CFT obligations.

d. One specialist training session on trade-based money laundering
   typologies, delivered to the compliance function in Q3 2025.

The 3 per cent gap in quarterly refresher completion reflects two
staff members on extended leave during Q2 2025. Both completed the
training on return.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. INTERNAL POLICY AND PROCEDURE CHANGES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two procedural changes were implemented during 2025:

a. In Q2 2025, the compliance function introduced an automated linked-
   transaction aggregation alert to reduce the delay between threshold
   breach and draft preparation.

b. In Q3 2025, the compliance function introduced a dormant file
   review reminder to identify stalled investigations.

No changes were made to the firm's AML and CFT policies during 2025.
The annual policy review was completed by the Board in December 2025.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. MATERIAL INCIDENTS AND EXCEPTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function recorded 41 incidents during 2025 (quarterly
breakdown: 9, 10, 11, 11). Of these:

Refused transactions:         18
Declined onboardings:          9
Compliance exceptions:        14

No incident resulted in a regulatory notification beyond the filing
activity already recorded above. The compliance function is satisfied
that each incident was recorded, investigated and, where applicable,
resolved through corrective action.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. MLRO VIEW OF PROGRAMME HEALTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I consider the firm's AML and CFT programme to be operating at a
satisfactory level. The compliance function has grown in capability
during the year, as reflected in improved detection rates and the
introduction of two procedural improvements. The filing throughput
is proportionate to the firm's business volume. The sanctions
screening programme is running without interruption. The PEP
monitoring programme is current.

I confirm that I have had unrestricted access to the books, records,
staff and resources of the firm during the year. I have not been
prevented from discharging any part of my function. There are no
matters that I consider necessary to bring to the attention of the
supervisory authority at this time.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. KEY RISKS CARRIED INTO NEXT YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

a. The recycled gold typology remains the firm's highest-priority risk
   area. The compliance function expects this typology to remain active
   in 2026.

b. High cash intensity at Entity Echo requires continued monitoring
   and may warrant a formal review of cash handling controls in Q1 2026.

c. The geographic concentration in the UAE, particularly in Ras Al
   Khaimah, warrants continued monitoring through the quarterly
   jurisdiction heatmap.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
13. RECOMMENDATIONS TO SENIOR MANAGEMENT AND THE BOARD FOR NEXT YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Approve the continuation of the current AML and CFT programme
   structure for 2026.

2. Note the key risks identified above and the compliance function's
   plan to address them.

3. Consider whether additional compliance resource is required to
   support the Entity Echo programme, given the rising cash intensity
   activity.

4. Approve the annual policy review schedule for 2026.

5. Confirm that filing mode for all five goAML filing types should
   remain set to manual for 2026.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
14. CLOSING
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I thank Senior Management and the Board for their continued support
of the firm's AML and CFT programme during 2025.
