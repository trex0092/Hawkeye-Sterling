1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS DOCUMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the draft annual enterprise-wide risk assessment of HAWKEYE
STERLING V2 for the calendar year 2025. It is prepared in discharge
of the firm's obligation under the applicable provisions of Federal
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 to maintain a documented risk assessment
covering the full scope of the firm's Money Laundering and Terrorism
Financing risk. The document is intended to be read, amended and
signed off by the Money Laundering Reporting Officer,
and subsequently presented to Senior Management and to the Board for
acknowledgement.

The risk assessment covers five risk pillars. For each pillar the
document sets out the relevant facts about the firm, the residual
risk rating assigned by the compliance function on a scale from low
to high, the principal controls in place, and the compliance
function's conclusions. The MLRO is invited to amend the risk ratings
where her professional judgement differs from the compliance
function.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. BUSINESS OVERVIEW
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Reporting Entity] is a UAE-licensed Dealer in Precious Metals and
Stones and is classified as a Designated Non-Financial Business and
Profession. The firm's supervisory authority for the purposes of
Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism is
the Ministry of Economy. The firm operates six active compliance
programmes across six legally distinct entities (Entity Alpha, Entity Bravo,
Entity Charlie, Entity Delta, Entity Echo and Entity Foxtrot) and a seventh
programme devoted to screening (SCREENINGS). The firm holds physical
branches in Dubai, Sharjah, Abu Dhabi and Ras Al Khaimah.

The firm's product and service offering consists of the buying and
selling of gold in the forms of bullion, jewellery scrap, old gold
and inherited gold, alongside recycled and refined material. The
firm does not currently offer custody services, safe deposit boxes,
loans against precious metals, or investment products, and this
assessment assumes that these product lines remain out of scope for
the period covered.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PILLAR 1 — CUSTOMER RISK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's customer population during 2025 consisted of approximately
3,100 unique customer files across the six programmes. Of these, 14
are classified as Politically Exposed Persons and are under Enhanced
Due Diligence. A further 87 customers are classified as medium to
high risk on the basis of their activity patterns, jurisdiction of
residence or source of funds. The balance of the population is
classified as standard risk.

The compliance function considers customer risk to be materially
driven by the recurring walk-in population on the recycled gold
business lines, where source-of-material evidence is sometimes weak
and where linked-transaction aggregation has materialised in
Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports in multiple months of
the year.

Residual customer risk rating: medium to high.

Principal controls in place: Customer Due Diligence at onboarding,
Enhanced Due Diligence for all PEPs and all customers on the
internal high-risk pathway, documented source of funds and source
of wealth for all customers above the firm's internal threshold,
linked transaction aggregation on a rolling 30 day window, and a
watch list of individuals who have failed or refused CDD in the
past.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PILLAR 2 — PRODUCT AND SERVICE RISK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's exposure is concentrated in the recycled gold and
jewellery scrap product lines. These product lines account for
approximately 62 percent of the firm's transaction volume by count
and approximately 48 percent by value during the year. The compliance
function regards these product lines as the highest risk within the
firm's current offering on the basis of published sector typologies
for Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones and on the basis of the
firm's own filing history during 2025.

Bullion and refined-material product lines are lower risk as the
material is typically acquired through approved refiners and the
source-of-material evidence is stronger.

Residual product and service risk rating: medium to high.

Principal controls in place: acceptance criteria for recycled gold
material, internal approved refiner list, secondary assay on flagged
batches, physical dual control in all branch safes, and a formal
escalation path from branch cashier to branch compliance officer to
the compliance function.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PILLAR 3 — GEOGRAPHIC RISK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's branches are located inside the United Arab Emirates,
which is the firm's primary jurisdiction. Cross-border exposure
arises where recycled gold material is evidenced by refiner
certificates originating outside the United Arab Emirates. During
2025 the firm recorded 41 transactions with a stated cross-border
refiner origin. Of these, 9 were from jurisdictions the compliance
function classifies as higher risk for conflict or recycled gold
sourcing.

The compliance function regards cross-border risk as a secondary but
growing exposure for the firm.

Residual geographic risk rating: medium.

Principal controls in place: refusal to accept material without a
verifiable origin, refiner verification against the firm's internal
approved refiner list, rejection of jurisdictions on the firm's
internal high-risk country list, and sanctions screening at the
transaction level against the UN Security Council Consolidated
List and the UAE Local Terrorist List.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PILLAR 4 — DELIVERY CHANNEL RISK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm does not offer remote onboarding. All customer relationships
are opened face to face at a branch. The firm's principal delivery
channel is the in-branch, walk-in and recurring-customer channel.
The firm does not currently process any transaction online, by
courier, or through intermediaries.

The compliance function regards delivery channel risk as low on the
basis of the exclusively in-branch model. The principal residual
risk in this pillar is the recurring walk-in customer, who can
attempt to defeat linked transaction aggregation by visiting different
branches on different days. This risk is mitigated by the firm's
enterprise-wide customer record.

Residual delivery channel risk rating: low to medium.

Principal controls in place: in-branch identification at every
transaction, shared enterprise-wide customer record, linked
transaction aggregation across branches, and an internal watch list
maintained across the six programmes.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PILLAR 5 — TYPOLOGY EXPOSURE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

During 2025 the firm filed 148 reports through the Financial
Intelligence Unit on the goAML platform (133 Dealers in Precious
Metals and Stones Reports, 9 Suspicious Transaction Reports, 3
Suspicious Activity Reports and 3 Partial Name Match Reports). The
filings distribute across the firm's typology catalogue in the
following way.

Linked cash transaction aggregation: 133 filings, all Dealers in
Precious Metals and Stones Reports.

Recycled gold sourcing and certification: 6 Suspicious Transaction
Reports and 2 Partial Name Match Reports.

Cross-border smuggling indicators: 2 Suspicious Transaction Reports
and 1 Partial Name Match Report.

Attempted onboarding without Customer Due Diligence: 3 Suspicious
Activity Reports.

Sanctions nexus: 0 Funds Freeze Reports. No match against either
list has been confirmed during the year.

The compliance function regards the recycled gold sourcing typology
as the firm's most material typology exposure for 2025. The compliance
function also regards the first quarter of 2026 as showing early
signs of a coordinated recycled gold sanctions exposure, which
warrants elevated attention during the period in which this risk
assessment will be in force.

Residual typology exposure rating: medium to high.

Principal controls in place: the firm's internal typology catalogue,
the weekly pattern report, the cross-entity counterparty register,
the MLRO's personal review of every filing, and the annual training
programme.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. OVERALL RESIDUAL RISK
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Taking the five pillars together, the compliance function assesses
the overall residual AML and CFT risk of [Reporting Entity] for the
year 2025 as medium to high. The principal drivers of this rating
are the customer risk and the product and service risk, both of
which reflect the recycled gold and jewellery scrap business lines.
The compliance function considers that the firm's existing control
framework is adequate at current activity levels but notes that the
recycled gold sanctions track in early 2026 has the potential to
change the rating materially if it escalates into a confirmed match.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. PROPOSED ACTIONS ARISING FROM THIS ASSESSMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function recommends the following eight actions arising
from this assessment, for the MLRO's consideration and, on her
approval, for submission to Senior Management.

a. Strengthen the acceptance criteria for recycled gold material to
   require a verifiable origin certificate from an approved refiner
   for every batch above an internal threshold.

b. Review the internal approved refiner list quarterly rather than
   annually, starting in the second quarter of 2026.

c. Open a formal review of cash handling controls on the Entity Echo
   programme in the first half of April 2026.

d. Introduce a dedicated cross-entity counterparty file for any
   counterparty that appears on the compliance records of two or
   more entities in the same rolling 30 day window.

e. Refresh the firm's internal typology catalogue during the second
   quarter of 2026 to reflect the recycled gold sanctions track and
   any related typologies observed during the year.

f. Refresh the annual sanctions training module to include a worked
   example drawn from the March 2026 Partial Name Match activity,
   once the file is closed and the facts are stable.

g. Review the firm's resourcing of the compliance function in light
   of the expected workload from the recycled gold sanctions track.

h. Present this risk assessment to the Board at the next standing
   meeting with a specific request that the Board formally
   acknowledge the recycled gold sanctions track as an agenda item.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. LIMITATIONS OF THIS ASSESSMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This assessment covers the calendar year 2025 and the opening weeks
of 2026. It reflects the activity recorded on the firm's internal
systems and the typology catalogue maintained by the compliance
function. It does not reflect any non-public intelligence received
from supervisory authorities after the date of drafting and should
be re-read if and when new supervisory guidance is published.
