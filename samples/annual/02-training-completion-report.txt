1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This report records the AML and CFT training delivered to staff of
[Reporting Entity] during the calendar year 2025, the population of
staff to whom each module was delivered, the completion rate, and the
competence assessment outcome. It is produced in discharge of the
firm's obligation to maintain an annual training programme for staff
whose functions expose them to Money Laundering or Terrorism Financing
risk, and to maintain records of that training for 10 years under the
applicable provision of Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025. The
training programme is designed in alignment with the Financial Action
Task Force (FATF) Recommendations, in particular Recommendation 22
(customer due diligence for DNFBPs) and Recommendation 23 (other
measures for DNFBPs), which require ongoing training on ML/TF risks
and obligations.

The report is prepared for the MLRO's review and subsequent
presentation to Senior Management and the Board.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

During 2025 the firm delivered four scheduled training modules and
two refresher sessions, covering a total of 41 unique members of
staff across the six active programme branches and the head office.
All 41 staff members completed the modules required for their role
by the end of the year. The overall completion rate on required
modules was 100 percent. No staff member carried an outstanding
training requirement into 2026.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. TRAINING MODULES DELIVERED DURING THE YEAR
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Module 1. AML and CFT Foundations for Dealers in Precious Metals and
Stones.
Delivered on 10 March 2025. In-person, head office, full day. Required
for all staff. Attendees: 38. Completion rate: 100 percent.
Competence assessment: short written test, pass mark 70 percent.
Pass rate: 100 percent (38 of 38).

Module 2. Targeted Financial Sanctions, including UN Security Council
Consolidated List and UAE Local Terrorist List.
Delivered on 05 June 2025. In-person, head office, half day. Required
for all staff in customer-facing or transaction-handling roles.
Attendees: 23. Completion rate: 100 percent. Competence assessment:
scenario walkthrough. Pass rate: 100 percent (23 of 23).

Module 3. Customer Due Diligence and Enhanced Due Diligence refresher.
Delivered on 14 September 2025. In-person, head office, half day.
Required for all branch compliance officers and branch managers.
Attendees: 14. Completion rate: 100 percent. Competence assessment:
written case study. Pass rate: 100 percent (14 of 14).

Module 4. Sanctions Screening and the goAML Filing Workflow.
Delivered on 05 December 2025. In-person, head office, full day.
Required for the MLRO, the Deputy MLRO, the compliance function, and
all branch compliance officers. Attendees: 12. Completion rate: 100
percent. Competence assessment: end-to-end mock filing. Pass rate:
100 percent (12 of 12).

Refresher 1. Red Flag Recognition for the Recycled Gold Business Line.
Delivered on 21 July 2025. In-person, delivered at each programme
branch over the course of one week. Required for branch cashiers and
branch compliance officers. Attendees: 29 across the six branches.
Completion rate: 100 percent.

Refresher 2. Linked Transaction Aggregation under the revised internal
procedure.
Delivered on 21 March 2025. In-person, head office, one hour. Required
for branch cashiers. Attendees: 19. Completion rate: 100 percent.

5. RECORDS RETAINED
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

For each training event delivered during 2025 the compliance function
retains the following records for ten years in accordance with the
applicable provision of Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025.

a. Signed attendance sheet for the session.
b. Copy of the training material as delivered on the day.
c. Competence assessment materials and each attendee's individual
   result.
d. A short summary prepared by the trainer of any questions raised by
   attendees during the session and the answers given.
e. Any correspondence with external trainers where the session was
   delivered by a third party.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. LIMITATIONS OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This report covers scheduled AML and CFT training delivered during the
calendar year 2025. It does not record informal conversations, memos
or short briefings delivered to individual staff members in the course
of supervision. Those are recorded in supervisor records separately
and are not aggregated here.
