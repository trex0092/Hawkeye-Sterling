1. PURPOSE AND STANDING OF THIS REPORT
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the quarterly report of the Money Laundering Reporting Officer
of [Reporting Entity] for the first quarter of 2026. It is prepared
for Senior Management and for the Board in support of the continuing
duty of the firm to maintain a Money Laundering Reporting Officer and
to ensure the governing body is informed of the operation of the firm's
AML and CFT programme. It is an internal report. It has not been filed
with any supervisory authority. It is retained in the firm's archive
for 10 years.

This report consolidates the three monthly MLRO reports issued during
the quarter and adds a quarterly trend narrative, a filings summary,
and a set of recommendations to Senior Management and the Board.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HEADLINE FOR THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm's compliance programme during the first quarter of 2026 was
characterised by steady operational throughput and one emerging risk
that will require sustained attention into the second quarter. The
emerging risk is the recycled gold sanctions typology that appeared on
three programme entities in March 2026 and which is expected to result
in at least one regulator-facing filing during April 2026. I am
satisfied that the compliance function identified the risk promptly
and that the matter is under appropriate management.

There are no matters on which I, in my capacity as MLRO, have been
unable to obtain the resources or the authority I need to discharge
my function during the first quarter of 2026.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. QUARTERLY FILINGS SUMMARY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm filed, through the Financial Intelligence Unit on the goAML
platform, the following reports during the first quarter of 2026:

Suspicious Transaction Reports filed:              4
Suspicious Activity Reports filed:                 2
Dealers in Precious Metals and Stones Reports:     31
Partial Name Match Reports filed:                  2
Funds Freeze Reports filed:                        0
                                                   --
Total filings through goAML during Q1 2026:        39

The filing volume is consistent with the quarterly baseline established
in the second half of 2025 (35 to 42 filings per quarter). The four
Suspicious Transaction Reports concerned recycled gold counterparties
on the Entity Alpha, Entity Charlie and Entity Delta programmes. The
two Suspicious Activity Reports concerned attempted onboardings that
did not result in completed transactions.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. QUARTERLY TREND NARRATIVE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

January 2026 was a routine month. The firm processed standard volume
across all six programmes and there were no material incidents. February
2026 saw an increase in the number of Dealers in Precious Metals and
Stones Reports, reflecting seasonal trading activity at the Entity Echo
and Entity Bravo branches. March 2026 saw the emergence of the recycled
gold sanctions typology on three entities and a modest rise in high
cash intensity activity at Entity Echo.

                                 --      --      --       --

The upward trend in monthly filings from 11 to 15 is attributable
primarily to the DPMSR volume and to the sanctions-related filings in
March. I do not consider this trend to be a cause for concern at this

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. MATTERS UNDER REVIEW AT QUARTER-END
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

a. The recycled gold sanctions exposure on the Entity Alpha, Entity
   Foxtrot and Entity Delta files. The counterparties may be related.
   If so, this will be the firm's first coordinated sanctions risk event
   of the year. I expect to reach a filing decision during the first
   two weeks of April 2026.

b. The high cash intensity activity at Entity Echo. I intend to conduct
   a formal review of cash handling controls at the Entity Echo branch
   during the first half of April 2026.

c. One PEP file on Entity Alpha that fell outside its EDD refresh cycle
   during March 2026. The review was completed on 30 March 2026 and the
   file has been restored to the regular cycle.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SANCTIONS SCREENING ACTIVITY FOR THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm screened its counterparty register and active customer
population against the United Nations Security Council Consolidated
List and the UAE Local Terrorist List on each business day of the
quarter. No confirmed matches were identified. Three medium-confidence
matches were reviewed and resolved as false positives. One medium-
confidence match (the RF-R12 counterparty) remains under review at
quarter-end.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PEP POPULATION MOVEMENT DURING THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The PEP population at 31 March 2026 stands at 14 files across 6
programmes. One new PEP designation was made during the quarter
(Entity Foxtrot, PEP-014, Silk Road Partners, designated in February
2026 on the basis of publicly available information). No PEP files
were closed or de-classified during the quarter.

All 14 PEP files are under Enhanced Due Diligence. The compliance
function confirms that 13 of the 14 files are within their EDD refresh
cycle. One file (PEP-002, Entity Alpha) fell outside the cycle during
March 2026, was reviewed on 30 March 2026, and has been restored.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. TRAINING DELIVERED DURING THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The firm delivered the following AML and CFT training during the
first quarter of 2026:

a. Q1 2026 AML Refresher Training. Completed by staff at Entity Alpha,
   Entity Bravo, Entity Echo and Entity Foxtrot. Entity Charlie and
   Entity Delta are scheduled for completion by mid-April 2026.

b. Sanctions Awareness Module 2026. Completed by Entity Alpha staff
   in March 2026. Scheduled for remaining entities during April and
   May 2026.

c. One new joiner induction at Entity Echo, covering the firm's AML
   and CFT policies and the new joiner's obligations under the
   applicable provisions of Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. INTERNAL POLICY OR PROCEDURE CHANGES DURING THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

No changes were made to the firm's AML and CFT policies during the
first quarter of 2026. One procedural change was implemented: the
compliance function added an automated alert to the Entity Echo cash
monitoring workflow in March 2026 following a compliance exception
identified in the monthly incident log.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. MATERIAL INCIDENTS OR EXCEPTIONS DURING THE QUARTER
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The compliance function recorded a total of 12 incidents during the
quarter (4 in January, 3 in February, 5 in March). Of these, 5 were
refused transactions, 3 were declined onboardings, and 4 were
compliance exceptions. None resulted in a regulatory notification
beyond the filing activity already recorded above. Full details are
set out in the monthly incident logs for each month.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. RISK ASSESSMENT AND PROGRAMME HEALTH
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I consider the firm's AML and CFT programme to be operating at a
satisfactory level for the first quarter of 2026, consistent with
the risk-based approach required by the Financial Action Task Force
(FATF) Recommendations and as implemented through Federal Decree-Law
No. 10 of 2025. The compliance
function is identifying risks in a timely manner and the filing
throughput is consistent with the quarterly baseline. The one area
that requires my sustained attention in the coming quarter is the
recycled gold sanctions exposure, which I will address as a priority.

I confirm that I have had unrestricted access to the books, records,
staff and resources of the firm during the quarter. I have not been
prevented from discharging any part of my function.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
